Read this first — an honest note Astrology has no demonstrated predictive validity and is classed by science as a pseudoscience (see Sources & Science for the evidence). This site presents Lilly's astrology and the Picatrix's astral magic as landmarks of intellectual and cultural history, and as fascinating formal systems to learn and compute. The mathematics is real and verifiable; the interpretations — dignities, judgements, elections, talismans, "magic" — are the historical doctrine, offered for study and described, never prescribed. The colour badges (green/amber/red) are a gravity scale for a verdict, not a claim about reality.

Built on verified astronomy

Every chart is computed in the browser from the astronomy-engine (truncated-VSOP87, MIT-licensed, ≈1 arc-minute). Angles and house cusps are computed here and checked against published reference vectors.

Validation (run on every change): mean obliquity matches to five decimals; the Sun is on 0° Aries at the equinox and 0° Cancer at the solstice; the Regiomontanus cusps come out strictly in order; the dignity tables reproduce Lilly's 1647 text; and ~80 engine assertions plus a real-browser sweep of every page must pass before anything ships.

Faithful, cited & honest

The doctrine follows Lilly's first edition (1647) and the Picatrix/Agrippa for the magic, with every rule carrying its source and contested points (the Part of Fortune by sect, the hyleg, the via combusta bounds, the operation→planet mapping) flagged, not hidden.

Full citations, the publishing history, the modern traditional revival and the scientific assessment are on the About & Sources page; every term is defined in the Glossary.
