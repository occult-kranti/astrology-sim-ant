Read this first — an honest note Astrology has no demonstrated predictive validity and is classed by the scientific community as a pseudoscience (see the Sources & Science page for the evidence — Carlson's 1985 Nature double-blind test, the “time-twins” study, and more). This project presents Lilly's astrology as a landmark of intellectual and cultural history, and as a fascinating formal system to learn and compute. The calculations are real and verifiable; the interpretations are Lilly's, offered for study — not as guidance.

The Three Books

An Introduction to Astrology

The alphabet of the art: the twelve signs, the seven planets, the twelve houses, the aspects, and the all-important tables of essential dignity. With an interactive dignity calculator and planetary-hours tool.

 Book II

The Resolution of All Questions

Horary astrology — answering a question from the chart of the moment it is asked. The considerations before judgement, the significators, the perfecting aspects, and Lilly's own famous worked charts. Includes a full horary calculator.

 Book III

The Judgement of Nativities

Natal astrology: rectification, the giver of life (hyleg), the Lord of the Geniture, temperament, and prediction by directions, profections and revolutions — with a nativity calculator.

Built on verified astronomy

Every chart is computed in the browser from the astronomy-engine (a truncated-VSOP87 model, MIT-licensed, accurate to about one arc-minute). The angles and house cusps are computed here and checked against published reference vectors.

Validation: mean obliquity matches the standard value to five decimal places; the Sun falls on 0° Aries at the equinox and 0° Cancer at the solstice; it sits on the Midheaven at local noon; and the twelve Regiomontanus cusps come out strictly in order. Tables of dignity reproduce Lilly's 1647 text (every term-row sums to 30°).

Faithful & cited

The text follows Lilly's first edition (1647), cross-checked against the 1659 second edition and the modern Regulus (1985) and Astrology Classics editions. Where editors or later authors disagree with Lilly — the orbs, the triplicity rulers, the Part of Fortune by sect — we follow what Lilly actually printed and flag the difference.

Full citations, the publishing history, the modern traditional revival, and the scientific assessment are on the About & Sources page.
