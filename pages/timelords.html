Time-lords & progressions — who "rules" the years of a life

Three classical answers to the same question — which planet governs this stretch of a life? — computed side by side on the site's verified engine: the secondary progressions of the day-for-a-year rule (Valens, Anthology IX.3, angles by the Naibod arc), the Persian firdaria periods of Abu Maʼshar (75-year cycle, with the disputed night-node placement exposed as an option), and Vettius Valens's zodiacal releasing from the Lots of Spirit and Fortune (360-day years, the loosing of the bond).

Honest note — please read These are historical timing doctrines, reproduced faithfully for study. The astronomy underneath (ephemeris positions, angles, the Lots) is real and verifiable; the idea that a planet "rules" a period of a life is a symbolic convention with no demonstrated predictive validity — astrology is regarded by the scientific community as a pseudoscience. Where the tradition itself disagrees (the firdaria night-node order, the year-length conventions), the fork is flagged in the data and shown, never silently chosen. Described, never prescribed. How this works — the three techniques, step by step

Secondary progressions (day-for-a-year)

The classical statement is Vettius Valens, Anthology IX.3 (trans. M. Riley): "add a number of days to the birth date equivalent to the age (in years) of the native… cast a horoscope for that day." The modern convention computed here: the progressed instant = birth instant + age in days, where the age is counted in tropical years (elapsed time ÷ 365.2422 d). The seven planets are read from the real ephemeris at that instant — one day of sky after birth stands for one year of life. The angles are progressed by the Naibod arc only: the Sun's mean daily motion, 360° ÷ 365.2422 = 0.98564733° = 59′08″ per year (Valentin Naibod, 16th c. — the same key as this site's primary directions); the progressed MC = natal MC + arc, and the progressed Ascendant is re-derived from that MC through the birth latitude and natal obliquity. Recognized alternatives (solar-arc MC, solar arc in right ascension, mean quotidian) are out of scope. The formalization of the day-for-a-year technique is conventionally credited to Placidus (17th c.), with Salmasius a rival claimant — an attribution question, not a settled fact.

Firdaria (al-firdārāt)

Life is divided into a repeating 75-year cycle of major periods (Abu Maʼshar, On the Revolutions of the Years of Nativities, trans. B. Dykes, Persian Nativities IV). A day birth runs Sun 10 · Venus 8 · Mercury 13 · Moon 9 · Saturn 11 · Jupiter 12 · Mars 7 · North Node 3 · South Node 2; a night birth starts from the Moon (Moon 9 · Saturn 11 · Jupiter 12 · Mars 7 · Sun 10 · Venus 8 · Mercury 13, then the nodes). The disputed point, flagged in the data and exposed as the toggle above: Abu Maʼshar as read by Dykes, Rob Hand and Steven Birchfield puts the nodes at the end of the night sequence (ages 70–75) — the default here — while Bonatti (Book of Astronomy), followed by Robert Zoller and several modern calculators, inserts them after Mars (ages 39–44). Day charts are identical under both. Each planetary major period divides into 7 equal sub-periods, the first ruled by the major lord itself, continuing in the firdaria order and skipping the nodes; the node periods are undivided. Dates here use Julian years (365.25 d) — an implementation convention, not a sourced rule.

Zodiacal releasing

Valens, Anthology IV.4–IV.10 (trans. M. Riley): periods are "released" through the signs from the whole sign holding the Lot of Spirit (Daimon — employment, rank, action) or the Lot of Fortune (the body); the degree is irrelevant. Each sign lasts its ruler's minor years — Aries 15, Taurus 8, Gemini 20, Cancer 25, Leo 19, Virgo 20, Libra 8, Scorpio 15, Sagittarius 12, Capricorn 27 (Valens derives it as half of half of the Moon's 108 — not 30), Aquarius 30, Pisces 12; the twelve sum to 211. The clock runs on Valens's own units (IV.9–IV.10): the distribution year is 360 days, and each level is 1/12 of the one above — L1 = number × 360 d, L2 = number × 30 d, L3 = number × 2.5 d, L4 = number × 5 h — so period boundaries drift against calendar years (a Cancer L1 beginning 2012-01-01 ends 9 000 real days later on 2036-08-22). Sub-periods start from their parent's own sign and proceed zodiacally, truncated at the parent's end; when a subdivision completes all 12 signs and would repeat its start, the 13th period instead jumps to the opposite sign — the loosing of the bond (R. Schmidt's term), possible only inside L1 signs whose number is ≥ 19 (Gemini, Cancer, Leo, Virgo, Capricorn, Aquarius), since a full L2 cycle = 211 months = 17 y 7 m. Valens's doctrinal swap rule — if Fortune or its ruler is badly situated, Spirit distributes both — is recorded as a note; this tool computes both releasings and leaves that judgement to the reader.
