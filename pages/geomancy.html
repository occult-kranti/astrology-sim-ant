Geomancy — the Shield Chart, cast & judged

Geomancy — ʿilm al-raml, "the science of the sand" — is the divinatory art Agrippa called the daughter of astrology. You strike four hasty rows of marks to raise the four Mothers; from them the engine derives the Daughters, the Nieces, the two Witnesses, the Judge and the Reconciler by pure algebra — and the Judge always comes out an even figure, the tradition's own built-in check that the working is sound. The twelve figures are then laid into the twelve houses and read by the house your question falls under.

Honest note — please read Geomancy is a historical divinatory art of no demonstrated validity. The mathematics of the shield is real and verifiable (it is Boolean algebra over sixteen figures); the interpretations are those of Agrippa (1533), Robert Fludd and the sand-geomancers, reproduced faithfully for study — never a prediction or advice. The figures are cast by your browser's random generator (or set by hand); nothing leaves the page.
