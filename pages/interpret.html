Reading your results — a reference guide

You have cast a chart on the Master Tool — now what do the panels mean? This is a plain guide to every block of the output, what its numbers and badges say, and how the tradition would weigh them. New to the words? Read The Basics first; for the arithmetic, How it's calculated.

Before you read a thing None of this predicts or determines anything in the real world — astrology has no demonstrated predictive validity. Read every panel as "this is what the tradition's rules say about this figure," a faithful reconstruction for study — described, never prescribed. Magical material is historical only.

How to work the tool

  1. Set the moment & place — pick a city (or 📍 Use my location), or type a latitude/longitude; set the date, time and UTC offset. Choose a house system if you have a preference (Regiomontanus is Lilly's).
  2. (Optional) add a birth moment — open "add a birth moment" to compute the life trajectory and the Vedic natal chart from an actual birth (the main moment still drives the "now" panels).
  3. Compute the reading — every panel fills at once. Toggle the 🕉 Vedic view (near the top) to see the sidereal chart side by side.
  4. Save it — Download JSON (the full data) or Download summary (MD) (a readable report); every reading is also auto-saved on your device. Copy shareable link puts the inputs in the URL.
  5. Ask Claude — at the foot, the assistant interprets the whole reading (see below).

The figure & the positions

The summary line gives the Ascendant and Midheaven, whether it's a day or night chart, the ruling planetary hour, and the chart-health badge. The positions table lists each body's longitude (e.g. 14°22′ Leo), its house, its speed, and a dignity total. A ℞ marks retrograde. The wheel draws the same thing; download it as SVG/PNG.

Book I — the dignity ledger

This scores each planet's strength. Read the signed numbers as a tally, not a percentage:

  • Essential — strength by sign/degree (domicile +5 … face +1; detriment −5, fall −4, peregrine −5). A high positive means the planet is "at home" and acts freely; a negative means it is weak or ill-placed.
  • Accidental — strength by situation (angular house, swift/direct motion, free of the Sun's beams, good contacts). Total = essential + accidental.
  • Almuten of the Ascendant/MC and the Lord of the Geniture (the most-dignified planet overall) name the chart's governing planets — the ones whose nature dominates the reading.

Rule of thumb: a planet strong by essential dignity does its own work well; strong by accidental dignity, it acts powerfully (for good or ill). The two can disagree — note it.

Aspects & reception

Each line is two planets in a significant angle: the aspect (trine, square…), whether it is applying (coming about) or separating (past/denied), and the orb (distance from exact — smaller is stronger; "partile" = exact to the degree). Reception lines show one planet sitting in another's dignity — a welcome that softens even a hard aspect (and mutual reception most of all).

Lots & antiscia · chart health

The Part of Fortune (⊕) and Part of Spirit are computed points for material and active fortune; the antiscia table gives each planet's "shadow" degrees — hidden sympathies.

The Chart health panel is the headline verdict — green / amber / red — a traffic-light scale for the gravity of the judgement, with the specific cautions beneath it (a void Moon, a combust significator, a via-combusta Moon…). It is a quick summary of impediments, not a substitute for weighing the actual significators — and red marks severity only, never a planetary or talisman colour.

Horary — the significators

By default the panel shows the general state: the querent's significator (the Lord of the Ascendant) and the Moon's condition and next applying aspect. Pick the house your question is about (the in-panel dropdown) to add the quesited's significator and the modes of perfection — whether and how the matter completes (a direct aspect, translation, collection, reception) or fails (prohibition, refranation), with an estimated timing.

Picatrix — election & talisman

  • Election — for the chosen aim, a verdict and a score with a cited breakdown, then all aims ranked for this moment. A hard requirement unmet (a "gating" line — e.g. the ruler retrograde or combust) keeps the verdict from green however high the score. The Picatrix correspondences of the moment give the Moon's mansion, the decan faces and any Behenian star contact.
  • Talisman — the ruling planet, its materials (suffumigation, colour, metal, stone), the named spirits (three systems kept distinct), the planet's historical Picatrix prayer, and the cited steps. Historical practice only — the recipe is recorded, never recommended; flagged materials must never be made.

Book III — the life trajectory

If you added a birth moment: the natal signatures (Lord of the Geniture, temperament, hyleg/alcocoden), this year's profection and Lord of the Year, and the native's ruling planets. The full year-by-year timeline, directions and solar return are on the Life Trajectory tool.

The Vedic (🕉) panel

A separate, sidereal reading — never merge it with the Western chart; compare them.

  • Grahas table — each graha's sidereal position, bhāva (whole-sign house), nakṣatra & pada, and Parāśarī dignity (Exalted / Own / Mūlatrikoṇa = strong; Debilitated = weak).
  • Pañcāṅga — the day's tithi, vāra, nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa. Vimśottarī daśā — the running mahā (and antar) period that times the life.
  • Navāṁśa & Sarvāṣṭakavarga — the D9 signs and the SAV bindu counts by sign (total always 337; >28 per sign is a strong field).
  • Ṣaḍbala — the six-fold strength per graha in rūpas, against a required minimum (ratio ≥ 1× = "strong"), with Iṣṭa (benefic yield) vs Kaṣṭa (its difficult counterpart). The weakest graha is the remedial focus.
  • Practice — the day's and the birth's traditional mantra, yoga and yantra. Cultural/devotional practice, recorded for study, never prescribed; the graha→āsana map is a modern syncretism, flagged.
  • Conclusions & advice — a computed plain-English synthesis of the above, ending in the honest caveat.
