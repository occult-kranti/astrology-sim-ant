I Ching — the Book of Changes, cast & read

The Yijing — the Book of Changes — answers a question by a hexagram cast at the moment of asking. Six lines are thrown (each young or old, yin or yang); the static lines give the primary hexagram (the situation), any moving lines are its pivots, the inner lines give the nuclear hexagram (the hidden core), and flipping the moving lines gives the relating hexagram — the direction the situation tends. All 64 figures are the King Wen sequence over the eight trigrams.

Honest note — please read The I Ching is a historical divinatory art of no demonstrated validity. The hexagram structure is exact, verifiable mathematics (the 64 figures are the 2⁶ combinations of six lines); the meanings are the tradition's, reproduced for study — never a prediction or advice. The line texts here are original paraphrases after James Legge (1899, public domain), not the copyrighted Wilhelm–Baynes translation. The coins are thrown by your browser's random generator; nothing leaves the page.
