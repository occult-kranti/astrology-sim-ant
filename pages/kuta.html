Kūṭa matching — the aṣṭakūṭa & the ten porutham

Please read first — a sensitive domain This tool reproduces a cultural matching custom: the North-Indian aṣṭakūṭa (36-guṇa milāpa) and the South-Indian ten porutham, scored from two people's Moon positions. It reports only what the tradition computes — it does NOT measure compatibility, does NOT predict a marriage, and is NOT advice about any real relationship. The gendered "groom/bride" roles and the traditional "consequences" of a rajju or nāḍī match are recorded as the tradition's own claims, presented as belief, never as fact about people. Like all astrology it has no demonstrated validity. Described, never prescribed. If you are making a real decision about a relationship, nothing here should inform it. See Sources & Science.

Guṇa-milāpa compares the two Moons: from each Moon's nakṣatra and rāśi the North scores eight kūṭas to a total of 36 (varṇa 1, vaśya 2, tārā 3, yoni 4, graha-maitrī 5, gaṇa 6, bhakūṭa 7, nāḍī 8), while the Tamil South checks ten poruthams with rajju and vedhā as vetoes. This lays both out with every rule cited and every genuine source-disagreement flagged (⚑) rather than silently resolved — including the one place the two systems flatly contradict each other (the 5/9 rāśi relation).

How this works — the kūṭas, step by step

Everything is read from the two Moons

Each birth chart is cast on the site's engine and the sidereal Moon taken (tropical − Lahiri ayanāṁśa); its nakṣatra (1–27, via nakshatraOf) and rāśi (⌊sid/30⌋) drive every kūṭa. No other planet is used. Boy = groom, girl = bride, because the classical matrices are keyed that way (the yoni, vaśya and gaṇa grids are asymmetric — the orientation is pinned to saravali, whose tables head the bride down the side).

The eight kūṭas

Varṇa (1) — the Moon-rāśi caste, groom's rank ≥ bride's. Vaśya (2) — the five-class control grid. Tārā/Dina (3) — the star-count mod 9, both ways, penalising the Vipat/Pratyari/Vadha tārās. Yoni (4) — the 27→14 animal table and its 14×14 matrix (the seven sworn-enemy pairs score 0). Graha- maitrī (5) — the two rāśi lords' naisargika friendship (BPHS 3.55–57, reused from the Vedic engine — aṣṭakūṭa ignores temporal friendship). Gaṇa (6) — deva/manuṣya/rākṣasa temperament. Bhakūṭa (7) — the mutual moon-sign counts, zeroed on 2/12, 5/9 or 6/8 (bhakūṭa-doṣa). Nāḍī (8) — the three pulse-groups; the same nāḍī scores 0 (nāḍī-doṣa, the heaviest single kūṭa).

Where the sources disagree — flagged, never resolved

Several cells are genuinely contested, and the tool keeps both readings: the varṇa air/earth swap (saravali gives the air signs Vaiśya, the common table gives them Śūdra); the graha-maitrī friend+enemy / neutral+enemy values (saravali 2/1 vs the common 1/0.5); the gaṇa grid orientation and spread (saravali's asymmetric 6/5/1 cells vs the common symmetric 5s and 0s); the bhakūṭa cancellation (same or friendly lords lift the doṣa — but whether the 7 points are restored is itself split, so we keep 0 by software convention and say so); and the nāḍī exempt-star lists (two distinct lists circulate even within one source — both are shown). Saravali's vaśya table genuinely omits Sagittarius; a flagged completion (Sag 1st half = Mānava, 2nd half = Catuṣpada, per the stevehora/classical scheme) lets a Sagittarius Moon be scored — and with that completion disabled the engine refuses to score rather than guess.

The ten porutham, and the contradiction

The Tamil South checks ten poruthams counted girl → boy. Its dina is a count-list, NOT the northern tārā (so count 10 passes the tārā but fails the Tamil dina — a divergence the tool reports). Rajju (the five body-groups) and vedhā (the twelve afflicting pairs + the {Mṛgaśīrṣa, Citrā, Dhaniṣṭhā} triple) are hard vetoes. And the two systems flatly contradict on one relation: a 5/9 moon-sign count is a 0-point bhakūṭa-doṣa in the North but a good rāśi porutham (the 5th/9th) in the South. The tool shows both verdicts side by side and resolves neither.
