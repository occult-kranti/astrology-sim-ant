Transits to a natal chart

A transit is where a planet is right now (or on any date) measured against a birth chart: the timing layer both contemporary traditional astrologers — Catherine Urban's "dates & degrees" calendars, Patrick Watson's transit work — lean on daily. For a nativity and a window, this finds the exact instants a transiting planet perfects a Ptolemaic aspect to a natal point (conjunction, sextile, square, trine, opposition), with applying/separating, the retrograde 1-or-3 pass structure, the planets' stations & ingresses, and — the classical join — the profection overlay: transits by the Lord of the Year, or to the Lord of the Year or the profected sign, are highlighted.

Honest note — please read Transits have no demonstrated predictive validity. The astronomy is real and reproducible — the hit-dates run on the same verified engine as every other tool, and each is solved to under a hundredth of a degree — but the meaning assigned to a transit is a historical symbolic convention, not a forecast. The rules are cited to Dorotheus (Carmen IV.1), Valens (Anthologies IV), Abū Maʿshar (Revolutions), Ptolemy (Tetrabiblos IV.10) and Lilly (Christian Astrology Bk III). The "time-lord activated" amplification — that a transit counts fully only when its planet is a current time-lord — is a modern-Hellenistic reconstruction (Brennan, 2017), labelled as such. See Sources & Science.

How this works

Finding an exact hit — a bisected zero-crossing

For each transiting planet, natal point and aspect the engine forms the signed offset d(t) = (transit longitude − natal longitude − aspect angle), wrapped to ±180°. An exact aspect is a zero-crossing of that function. A light single-vector longitude (the same geocentric apparent true-equinox-of-date path the rest of the site uses, ≈1 arc-minute) is sampled day by day across the window; each sign change of d — guarding the ±180° wrap — is bisected to the second (the reported offset re-computes to under a hundredth of a degree). The transiting Moon is excluded by default: at ~13°/day it perfects dozens of exact aspects a month and would drown the timeline; its rhythm is the lunar returns below, or tick it on to add it (then a 6-hour sub-step resolves its hits).

Retrograde 1-or-3 passes

A slower planet crossing a natal degree, then retrograding back over it, then turning direct and crossing a third time, gives the classic three passes of one aspect (Saturn's return, an outer-planet transit). Consecutive crossings of the same target within one retrograde loop are numbered pass 1 / 2 / 3 (the middle pass retrograde); crossings a whole orbital lap apart are separate single passes, never merged.

The profection overlay — the classical join, honestly labelled

The nativity's annual profection advances the Ascendant one whole sign per year of life; the domicile ruler of the profected sign is the Lord of the Year (Valens, Anthologies IV.11; Dorotheus, Carmen IV.1). The tradition judges the year from the profected sign, its lord, and the transits to/from these places (Dorotheus IV.1; Ptolemy, Tetrabiblos IV.10 — "the ingresses which are made to the places of the times"). A transit is time-lord activated here when the transiting planet is the Lord of the Year, when the natal point struck is the Lord of the Year, or when the natal point sits in the profected sign. The stronger claim — that a transit "counts" fully only when its planet is a current time-lord — is the modern-Hellenistic reconstruction of Chris Brennan (2017), reported as reconstruction, not asserted as ancient. A window can straddle a birthday, so more than one Lord of the Year may govern it; each event is judged by the profection in force at its own instant.

What is honestly out of scope

This lists when aspects perfect and flags the time-lord join; it does not interpret them. The tradition weighs each transiting planet's natal and revolutional condition, its sect, reception and dignity, the solar-revolution chart, and the direction/profection/revolution hierarchy above the transit (Abū Maʿshar, in Dykes, Persian Nativities IV, 2019) — layers of judgement no timeline can settle. The orbs of the snapshot are Lilly's planetary moieties; natal angles carry no moiety and are judged by the transiting planet's orb alone (flagged). None of it is evidence of anything.

A strong, honest caveat None of this predicts or determines anything. The transit dates are exact astronomy; the timing doctrine is a faithful reconstruction of how the tradition read a life against the moving sky — cited source by source, with the modern reconstruction of the "time-lord activated" filter labelled rather than smuggled in.
