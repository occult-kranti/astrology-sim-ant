The Ānāpānassati Sutta — mindfulness of breathing, MN 118

The canonical instruction on ānāpānassati — mindfulness of in-and-out breathing — set out as sixteen steps in four tetrads, and then mapped onto the four satipaṭṭhānas and the seven awakening factors. A prose discourse of 154 segments: each carries its Mahāsaṅgīti Pāli, a word-by-word gloss (hover, tap or keyboard-focus any word), and Sujato's verbatim CC0 translation.

Described, never prescribed A historical Buddhist meditation text, read closely for study. The instructions and their fruits — the tetrads, the awakening factors, "true knowledge and freedom" — are the text's own, reported here with no demonstrated validity as claims about the world, and are not presented as a practice to follow. The Pāli is public domain; the translation is CC0; the gloss prose is original (PED-informed).
On the refrains — the peyyāla Prose suttas repeat fixed blocks, and the manuscripts themselves abbreviate them with …pe…. Rather than gloss the same words dozens of times or silently drop them, this wing glosses each refrain once (at the top), and where it recurs shows it collapsed with a "show the full refrain" toggle. You see the structure of the text, never a hidden repetition.
