The Metta Sutta — Karaṇīyametta Sutta, Snp 1.8

The discourse on loving-kindness (mettā): ten short verses on the good-will to be radiated "to all beings" without measure. Sutta Nipāta 1.8, the eighth sutta of the Uragavagga — the same text recurs as Khuddakapāṭha 9. Each segment carries its Mahāsaṅgīti Pāli, a word-by-word gloss (hover, tap or keyboard-focus any word for its meaning, grammar and PED citation), and Bhikkhu Sujato's verbatim CC0 translation.

Described, never prescribed A historical Buddhist text, read closely for study. Its claims — the good of loving-kindness, the abandonment of "return to a womb" — are the text's own, reported here with no demonstrated validity as claims about the world. The gloss prose is original (PED-informed); the Pāli is public domain; the translation is CC0. Every line says which is which.
