The Heart Sūtra — Prajñāpāramitā-hṛdaya (shorter)

The shortest distillation of the Perfection-of-Wisdom literature — "form is emptiness, emptiness is form" — and the most-recited text in East-Asian Buddhism. This wing renders the shorter Sanskrit recension (Müller–Nanjio's 1884 edition of the Hōryū-ji palm-leaf manuscript) word-by-word, with F. Max Müller's public-domain 1894 translation alongside. Hover, tap or keyboard-focus any word for its meaning, grammar and a Monier-Williams citation.

Described, never prescribed A historical Mahāyāna text, read closely. Its central claims — that the five aggregates are "empty of self-nature" (svabhāva-śūnya), that the mantra "removes all suffering" — are the text's own, reported here with no demonstrated validity as claims about the world. The gloss prose is original (Monier-Williams-informed); the Sanskrit and the translation are both public domain.
