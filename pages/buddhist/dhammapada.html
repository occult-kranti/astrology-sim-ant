The Dhammapada — Dhammapada, vaggas 1–5 (Dhp 1–75)

The most beloved verse anthology of the Pāli canon — a book of the Khuddaka Nikāya. This reading room glosses the first five chapters (vaggas): the Yamakavagga "Pairs" (1–20), Appamādavagga "Heedfulness" (21–32), Cittavagga "the Mind" (33–43), Pupphavagga "Flowers" (44–59) and Bālavagga "the Fool" (60–75). Each segment carries its Mahāsaṅgīti Pāli, a word-by-word gloss (hover, tap or keyboard-focus any word for its meaning, grammar and PED citation), and Bhikkhu Sujato's verbatim CC0 translation. The chapter vatthu story-titles and vagga colophons are kept honestly untranslated where Sujato leaves them so.

Described, never prescribed A historical Buddhist text, read closely for study. Its claims — the fruits of a purified mind, the "deathless" (amata), rebirth and its ending — are the text's own, reported here with no demonstrated validity as claims about the world, and are not presented as a teaching to adopt. The Pāli is public domain; the translation is CC0; the gloss prose is original (PED-informed). Every line says which is which.
On the famous cruxes The Dhammapada's most argued lines — manopubbaṅgamā dhammā (1:1, does mind cause or merely precede the mental states?), appamādo amatapadaṁ (21, the "path to the deathless"), the archaic yamāmase (6:2, "let us restrain ourselves" vs "we too must die"), dīpa "island" or "lamp" — are given careful both-ways notes on the segment itself, and never resolved. Where a verse is repeated as a "pair" its Pāli is glossed again in full, not silently deduplicated.

How much of the Dhammapada is here Vaggas 1–5 of the traditional 26 (Dhp 1–75) are word-by-word'd in full — 395 Bilara segments, 957 glossed tokens. The remaining 21 vaggas (Dhp 76–423) are catalogued, not yet glossed; Bhikkhu Sujato's complete CC0 Dhammapada is freely readable at SuttaCentral, the same source this wing reproduces.
