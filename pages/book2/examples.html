Lilly's Worked Charts — read live

The enduring fame of Christian Astrology rests on its case studies — real questions Lilly judged, with the figure, his reasoning, and the outcome. Here the most famous are read live: each chart is fed from the positions Lilly printed in his 1647 woodcut into the same engine the calculators use, so you can watch the wheel drawn, every dignity scored, and the house-by-house judgement run — beside Lilly's own words and the recorded outcome.

Honest note — what “live” means here The wheel and every score are computed from the positions Lilly printed, not re-derived from a date (which would import his ephemeris and calendar error). Where the woodcut is hard to read, the uncertain planet is flagged in each chart's Sources & reconstruction notes. The wheel is drawn with equal houses from the printed Ascendant; each planet keeps Lilly's own house. Applying/separating uses canonical mean motions. Astrology has no demonstrated predictive validity — this teaches Lilly's method and its history, described never prescribed. See Sources & Science.
