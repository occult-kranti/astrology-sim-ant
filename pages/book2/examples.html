Lilly's Worked Charts

The enduring fame of Christian Astrology rests on its case studies — real questions Lilly judged, with the figure, his reasoning, and the outcome. Four of the most famous follow, with the technique each illustrates. (Quotations are from Lilly's text, spelling lightly modernised.)

7th house · theft · translation

Of a ship at sea — “if sunk, or living?”

The question (December 1644). A London merchant's ship was feared lost off Spain after great storms; the report of her loss was so general that no insurer would touch her. A friend asked Lilly what he thought.

The figure. 11°33′ Gemini ascending, with fixed stars of Saturn's nature rising. The Ascendant signifies the body of the ship; the Moon (lady of the Ascendant) signifies the company and the ship's life. The Moon was in her exaltation in Taurus, in the 11th house.

The reasoning. Though Saturn cast a square near the Ascendant — and “all planets opposite to the Ascendant in this judgment are dangerous” — the Moon, exalted and in the house of comfort, cast a sextile to the degree ascending and was applying to a trine of Venus and the Sun in an angle, swift in motion. The ship was “much of the nature of Saturn — a heavy one, of no good speed”, and had taken some bruise or leak.

Judgement & outcome. “I gave my opinion that the ship was not lost, but did live, though of late in some danger, and was now recovered” — and lay to the south-west, on the English coast, mending. Lilly added that news would come within a day or two. It proved true: she was in the west, in a harbour, and word arrived as predicted.

7th house · theft

The stolen fish — “who stole them, and shall they be recovered?”

The technique. In theft questions the thief is shown by a peregrine planet in an angle, or by the lord of the 7th; the stolen goods by the 2nd house and the Moon; the place they are hidden by the 4th. The planet signifying the thief describes his sex, age and complexion.

The reasoning. The significators fell in water signs — pointing to a thief who worked on the river and goods kept in a moist place — and the description (from Mars and Jupiter) gave a tall, well-built, fair, reddish-haired man. The Moon applying to Mercury (lord of the 2nd) promised recovery, but Mercury weak in Pisces meant only part would be regained.

Outcome. A fisherman matching the description was found by the water; with a warrant his house was searched, part of the fish recovered, and the thief confessed. Lilly adds, drily, that he never liked theft questions and judged them “only with intention to benefit posterity.”

7th house · marriage · translation of light

A lady — “shall she marry the gentleman desired?”

The question. A gentlewoman who had refused a long-time suitor now passionately wished the match back on, and asked whether it could be effected.

The figure. Leo ascending: the lady is the Sun (lord of the 1st), with the Moon as co-significatrix. The man is Saturn (lord of the 7th, Aquarius). The Moon's application to the Sun gave “little hope” of itself, and there was no reception between the two principals.

The key — translation. But Jupiter (a fortune, in his exaltation) was applying to a sextile of Saturn and then to the Sun — translating the light of the lord of the 7th to the lord of the Ascendant. “This was the main occasion, without which it could not have been effected.” Lilly advised her to begin afresh through a gentleman intermediary on a day when Saturn and Jupiter held an exact sextile.

Outcome. “My counsel was followed, and the issue was that by the gentleman's means the matter was brought on again, the match effected, and all within twenty days.”

6th house · lost beast · direction & timing

A dog missing — “where is he, and shall I recover him?”

The figure. The lost dog is signified by Venus in Libra — an airy western sign. The querent lived at Temple Bar.

The reasoning. Venus in a western sign put the dog westward of the owner — Lilly judged “about Long Acre, or the upper part of Drury Lane.” Venus in an airy sign, under the Sun's beams and with the benefics in the 8th, meant the dog was “in some chamber or upper room, kept privately.” The Sun's coming trine to Saturn (lord of the Ascendant) timed the recovery to the following Monday.

Outcome. The very same Monday, about ten in the morning, a gentleman who had found the dog chained under a table in Long Acre sent him home — “to my very great credit.”
