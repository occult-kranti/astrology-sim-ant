How to judge a question — Lilly's method, step by step

Erect the figure for the moment the question is understood, at your own place. Note the planetary hour.
Test radicality — the considerations before judgement. Is the Ascendant in fit degrees? Is the Moon void of course or in the Via Combusta? These are cautions for reflection, not absolute bars.
Assign the houses. The 1st house and the Moon always signify the querent. The house ruling the matter asked about signifies the quesited — money the 2nd, marriage or an enemy the 7th, career the 10th, and so on. For a question about a third party, “turn the chart” so that person's house becomes a new Ascendant.
Identify the significators. Querent = lord of the 1st, the Moon, and (for a man) the Sun / (for a woman) Venus. Quesited = lord of the relevant house, any planet in it, and its natural significator.
Weigh each significator by essential dignity (is it strong or peregrine?) and accidental state (angular or cadent? combust? retrograde?). The calculator scores all of this.
Look to the perfecting aspect between the two significators. An applying aspect promises the matter; a separating one denies it or shows it past. A conjunction or sextile/trine perfects readily; a square or opposition only with difficulty — unless eased by reception.
If they do not aspect, look for translation of light (a swift planet carrying virtue from one to the other) or collection of light (a heavier planet receiving both). Beware prohibition, refranation and frustration, which deny the matter.
Read the Moon as the general flow of the matter: the planet she last left shows the past, the planet she next applies to shows the outcome.
Time the event from the degrees between the significators to exactitude, scaled by the signs (movable = swift, fixed = slow, common = a mean) and the houses (angular = soonest, cadent = latest).

The modes of perfection

Direct perfection

Conjunction — the matter comes about “without any manner of let or impediment.” Sextile/Trine from good houses — with ease. Square/Opposition — only with labour, and best with mutual reception.

By a third planet

Translation of light: a light planet separates from one significator and applies to the other, bringing them together (often a go-between). Collection of light: both significators apply to a heavier planet that receives them, who “collects” the matter to perfection.

Reception

When each significator sits in a dignity of the other, there is mutual reception — the strongest of helps, bringing a matter to pass “suddenly, and to the content of both parties,” even through a hard aspect.

The denials

Prohibition — a third planet perfects with a significator first, blocking the matter. Refranation — a significator turns retrograde before the aspect completes. Frustration — the awaited planet joins another first. Combustion of a significator — the worst impediment.

A reminder Horary's central claim — that the sky at the moment of a question encodes its answer — has never passed a controlled test. Learn the method as a beautiful formal system and a piece of history; the About page sets out the evidence.
