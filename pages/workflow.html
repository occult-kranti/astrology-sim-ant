The horary workflow, step by step

Erect the figure for the moment the question is understood, at your own place; note the planetary hour.
Test radicality — the considerations. Early/late Ascendant? Moon void of course or in the Via Combusta? (Cautions, not bars.)
Assign the houses. 1st + Moon = querent; the matter's house = quesited. For a third party, turn the chart.
Identify the significators and weigh each by essential dignity and accidental state.
Find the perfecting aspect between them — applying promises, separating denies; reception eases a hard aspect.
If no direct aspect, look for translation or collection of light; beware prohibition and refranation.
Read the Moon for the general flow, then time the event from the degrees to perfection.
Worked example — Book II In the Horary Calculator, once the querent's and quesited's significators are set, the tool reports the mode of perfection. If, say, the Moon separates from the lord of the 1st and applies to the lord of the 7th, it reports translation of light — the Moon carrying the matter to its conclusion — and estimates the timing from the applying orb (e.g. about 4 weeks, a movable sign in a succedent house).

The Picatrix bridge — election & talisman

Lilly's astrology is the apparatus the Picatrix (the great medieval handbook of astrological magic) assumes for its elections — choosing a fortunate moment to act. A Picatrix manuscript demonstrably passed through Lilly's own circle (Forman → Napier → Ashmole → Lilly). The Election Engine on the roadmap reuses this site's dignity, Moon and planetary-hour machinery to answer a single question: "is this moment fit for operation X?", with the full caution list this site already computes. The reference systems it draws on are now built — see the Picatrix hub; it is presented as the history of a practice, not a recommendation.
