The Basics — every concept, explained

Start here if the words are new. Read this first — an honest note Astrology has no demonstrated predictive validity and is classed by science as a pseudoscience (see Sources & Science). Everything below is explained as the doctrine of a historical tradition — what astrologers and magicians believed and how they reasoned — so you can learn and compute the system. The mathematics is real; the interpretations are the tradition's, described, never prescribed. Magical material is recorded as history only, never as instruction.

The chart & the zodiac

A horoscope (or "figure", or "chart") is a map of the sky for one moment seen from one place on Earth. Cast it on the Master Tool by giving a date, a time and a location.

  • The zodiac — the band of sky along the Sun's yearly path, divided into twelve 30° signs (Aries … Pisces). In Lilly's tropical zodiac, 0° Aries is fixed to the spring equinox.
  • The signs carry an element (fire/earth/air/water) and a mode (movable/fixed/common), which colour how a planet there behaves. See the signs reference.
  • The Ascendant — the degree rising on the eastern horizon at that moment; the cusp of the 1st house and the chart's anchor (the querent, the body, the self). The Midheaven (MC) is the degree culminating overhead — honour, career, reputation.
  • The houses — the twelve segments of the local sky, fixed to the horizon, through which the signs and planets rotate. This site uses Lilly's Regiomontanus houses by default (Placidus, whole-sign and equal are also offered).

The planets & the lights

Traditional astrology uses seven planets — the two luminaries (Sun, Moon) and five "wandering stars" (Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn) — plus the Moon's two nodes. Each has a nature and a set of significations; full tables are in the planets reference.

  • The Sun — vitality, honour, the father, the king; the Moon — the body's flux, the mother, the common people, and (in horary) the co-significator of every matter.
  • Benefics & malefics — Jupiter (the Greater Fortune) and Venus (the Lesser) incline to good; Saturn (the Greater Infortune) and Mars (the Lesser) to harm; Mercury is "convertible," taking the colour of what it touches.
  • Retrograde — a planet apparently moving backward through the zodiac; the tradition reads it as hesitant, undoing or contrary (a debility).
  • The Dragon's Head & Tail (the lunar nodes ☊ ☋) — points, not bodies; the Head increases, the Tail diminishes.

The twelve houses

Each house governs a department of life. In horary the question decides which house matters; in a nativity all twelve are read. The full table (with joys and the questions each answers) is on the houses page; in brief:

1st self/body · 2nd wealth · 3rd siblings, short journeys · 4th home, father, land · 5th children, pleasure · 6th illness, servants · 7th marriage, partners, open enemies · 8th death, the other's money · 9th religion, long journeys, learning · 10th honour, career, the mother · 11th friends, hopes · 12th secret enemies, confinement, self-undoing.

Angular / succedent / cadent — houses 1·4·7·10 are angular (strongest, acting fast); 2·5·8·11 succedent (moderate); 3·6·9·12 cadent (weakest, slow). A planet's house is a chief part of its accidental dignity.

Aspects & reception

An aspect is a meaningful angle between two planets — the only way, in this system, that they "see" and act on one another. Lilly uses the five Ptolemaic aspects:

  • Conjunction (0°) — union; good or ill by the planets joined. Sextile (60°) — ease, friendship. Square (90°) — friction, obstruction. Trine (120°) — the strongest harmony. Opposition (180°) — open conflict, separation.
  • Orbs & moieties — an aspect "operates" within a body of light (orb) that Lilly assigns to planets, not to aspects; two planets are in aspect when within the sum of their two half-orbs (moieties).
  • Applying vs separating — applying (the faster planet moving toward exactness) means the matter is coming about; separating means it is past or denied. This distinction is the heart of horary timing.
  • Reception — when a planet sits in another's dignity, it is "received" (a welcome). Mutual reception — each in the other's dignity — is the strongest help, and can perfect a matter even through a hard aspect.

Essential & accidental dignity

"Dignity" measures a planet's strength — and it comes in two kinds. The Dignity Calculator scores both.

Essential dignity — strength by the sign and degree a planet occupies, as if a guest's standing in a house:

  • Domicile (+5) — its own sign, at home. Exaltation (+4) — an honoured guest (e.g. the Sun at 19° Aries). Triplicity (+3) — rulership of the element, by day or night. Term/Bound (+2) and Face/Decan (+1) — minor subdivisions.
  • Detriment (−5, the sign opposite its domicile) and Fall (−4, opposite its exaltation) are the debilities; a planet with no dignity anywhere it stands is peregrine (−5) — a wanderer with no standing.
  • The almuten of a degree is the planet with the most essential dignity there — its "victor" or lord. The Lord of the Geniture is the planet most dignified in the whole chart.

Accidental dignity — strength by situation: an angular house, swift and direct motion, freedom from the Sun's beams, and helpful contacts. The chief debilities are being combust (within ~8½° of the Sun, "burnt up"), retrograde, or besieged between the two malefics; the great fortitude is cazimi ("in the heart of the Sun," within 17′).

The Lots, sect & the planetary hours

  • The Part of Fortune (⊕) — a computed point (Ascendant + Moon − Sun) marking bodily and material well-being. It is the most-used of the Arabic Parts or Lots — affine points that "carry" a signification to a new place in the chart.
  • Antiscia — a degree's "shadow" reflected across the solstice axis; a hidden sympathy that acts like a soft aspect.
  • Sect — whether the chart is of the day (Sun above the horizon) or night. Sect decides which triplicity ruler applies and which planets are "in favour," and (by some authors) reverses the Part of Fortune by night.
  • Planetary days & hours — each day and each unequal "hour" between sunrise and sunset is ruled by a planet in the Chaldean order (Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon). The Planetary Hours tool shows the ruler now; it is the backbone of election and talismanic magic.

Horary — answering a single question

Horary (Lilly's Book II) answers a specific question from a chart cast for the moment it is understood. Try it on the Horary Calculator.

  • Querent & quesited — the asker (1st house & its lord, plus the Moon) and the thing asked about (the relevant house & its lord). The planets ruling them are the significators.
  • Considerations before judgement — Lilly's checks on whether a chart is "radical" (fit to judge): a too- early or too-late Ascendant, the Moon void of course or in the Via Combusta (15° Libra–15° Scorpio), Saturn in the 1st or 7th, and so on.
  • The modes of perfection — how a matter completes: a direct aspect of the significators; translation (a swift planet carries light between them); collection (a heavier planet gathers both); or by reception. Or it fails — by prohibition (a third planet intervenes first) or refranation (a significator turns retrograde before the aspect completes).
  • The Moon is always co-significator and shows the drift of the matter; her next applying aspect, and whether she is void of course, is read in every question. Timing is estimated from the degrees to perfection and the signs/houses involved.

Nativities — reading a whole life

Natal astrology (Lilly's Book III) reads the chart of a birth. The Nativity Calculator, Book III Master and the Life Trajectory tool compute it.

  • The Lord of the Geniture (the most dignified planet) and the temperament (the humoral balance — choleric, sanguine, melancholic, phlegmatic) describe the native's cast of character.
  • Hyleg & alcocoden — the "giver of life" and the "giver of years," a (contested) method for the indicated length of life by planetary years.
  • Profections — advancing the Ascendant one sign per year of life; the lord of the profected sign is the Lord of the Year, the planet that "rules" that year. Primary directions (here, a simplified Naibod key) and the solar return (the chart of the Sun's yearly return) add further timing.

The Picatrix — astral magic (historical)

The Picatrix (Ghāyat al-Ḥakīm, "The Goal of the Wise") is the great medieval handbook of talismanic magic. It shares Lilly's machinery and adds a doctrine of when and how to act. The Picatrix hub gathers it; presented strictly as historical practice — described, never prescribed.

  • The 28 lunar mansions — stations of the Moon's monthly path, each with a magical virtue and (in the tradition) a talismanic image. The 36 decan faces — 10° thirds of each sign, each with a ruler and an image. The 15 Behenian fixed stars — potent stars with their stones, plants and uses (precessed live).
  • Planetary correspondences — for each planet, the petitions it governs and its suffumigation, colour, metal, stone and named spirits — the raw material of a talisman.
  • Election — judging whether a moment is fit for a chosen aim, by dignity, the planetary hour, the Moon's condition and mansion, the faces and the star contacts. The Election Engine ranks any moment and scans for the best windows.
  • Talismans — an aim → an elected moment → materials, mansion and image → a consecration. The Talisman Workshop assembles a cited recipe card.
  • Prayers, spirits & the Perfect Nature (Books III–IV) — the seven planetary prayers, the planetary spirits, and the practitioner's personal celestial genius (the Perfect Nature) — recorded as historical text only.

The Vedic (Jyotiṣa) system — a second tradition

Alongside Lilly's Western chart, the site computes the Vedic (Jyotiṣa) horoscope as Jagannath Hora does. It is a separate system — a different zodiac and different methods — shown side by side for comparison (toggle the 🕉 view on any calculator).

  • Sidereal zodiac & ayanāṁśa — Vedic astrology measures against the fixed stars, not the equinox; a planet's sidereal longitude = its tropical longitude − the ayanāṁśa (≈24° today; this site uses Lahiri). This is the chief difference from Lilly's tropical chart.
  • Rāśis, the Lagna & bhāvas — the twelve sidereal signs, the Vedic Ascendant (Lagna), and the whole-sign houses (bhāvas). Grahas — the nine "seizers" (the seven planets + the nodes Rāhu & Ketu).
  • Nakṣatras — the 27 lunar mansions (13°20′ each); the Moon's nakṣatra seeds the daśā and the birth "star." The Pañcāṅga — the almanac's "five limbs": tithi, vāra, nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa.
  • The Vimśottarī daśā — a 120-year cycle of planetary periods (mahā) and sub-periods (antar) that times the life, apportioned from the birth Moon. The vargas — divisional charts (D1–D60) for specific life areas (D9 navāṁśa, D10 career…).
  • Aṣṭakavarga — a bindu (point) score grading each sign/bhāva. Ṣaḍbala — the "six-fold strength" of each graha. Yogas — named planetary combinations. The Vedic panel also lists the traditional daily and birth practice (mantra, yoga, yantra) — recorded as cultural practice, never prescribed.

What the colours mean

The green / amber / red badge The verdict badge is a traffic-light scale for the gravity of a judgement, not a quality of the planets: green few impediments — favourable; amber mixed — weigh the cautions; red strongly impeded — the testimonies stand against it. Red marks severity only — it is not the planetary or talisman colour red (e.g. Mars's red garment), which is a separate ritual correspondence. The badge is a crude summary — never a substitute for weighing the actual significators, which is the real method.
