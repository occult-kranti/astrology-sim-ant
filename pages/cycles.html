Cycles of History — great conjunctions & eclipses

Every ~20 years Jupiter overtakes Saturn: the great conjunction, the slowest beat the naked-eye sky keeps. Medieval astrologers, following Abū Maʿshar, made this the clock of world history — dynasties on the 20-year hand, religions on the ~240-year shift of triplicity, prophets on the ~960-year return — and Kepler drew its rotating trigon in 1606. This tool computes the real conjunctions across the centuries, lays the doctrine's idealized mean series beside them, and adds an honest eclipse finder built on the same "possibility" logic the Babylonians used.

Honest note — please read The astronomy on this page is real and checkable: the conjunctions, trigon shifts and eclipse windows are computed in your browser (≈1 arc-minute engine) and verified against published tables. The meanings — plagues, prophets, the fates of kings — are documented historical beliefs with no demonstrated validity, reproduced for study with their sources. The two most famous cases below are a debunked cause (the Black Death) and a disputed identification (the Star of Bethlehem). Described, never prescribed.

How this works

Finding the conjunctions

The engine samples the signed difference lon(Jupiter) − lon(Saturn) in ~20-day steps and watches for a change of sign — a zero crossing. (The ±180° jump at opposition is also a sign change, so crossings are only accepted where the difference moves by a few degrees, not ~360°.) Each crossing is then bisected to under an hour. Because the difference moves at most ~0.25°/day, a 20-day step cannot skip a conjunction. Near opposition, apparent retrogradation can make Jupiter pass Saturn three times in ~6–7 months — the famous triple conjunctions (1940–41, 1980–81, 7 BCE; next 2238–39). The quoted separation is the minimum great-circle distance sampled ±10 days around the crossing: the planets differ in ecliptic latitude, so they pass near each other, never through.

Which longitudes these are

All positions are geocentric, apparent, tropical of-date (true equinox of date) — the frame of the zodiac signs and of R. Nolle's published conjunction table, against which all thirteen reference conjunctions on this page were verified to the arcminute. Fixed-epoch (J2000) tables such as Wikipedia's differ by accumulated precession, ≈1.396°/century, and date their rows by minimum separation rather than the longitude crossing — two honest conventions that disagree by a degree or two and a day or two. Conjunctions in right ascension fall on different days again.

Mean vs geocentric — the doctrinal overlay

Abū Maʿshar's historical astrology runs on mean conjunctions computed from mean motions: exactly one every 19.859 Julian years, each +242.98° (tropical) from the last, twelve per triplicity, 960 years round the cycle — an idealized clock that never produces a triple. The overlay is anchored at the engine-verified 2020-12-21 crossing and stepped by exactly those constants. Comparing it with the computed timeline shows where doctrine and sky part company — e.g. the 1980–81 Libra triple, an air foray inside the earth series the mean clock knows nothing about.

The eclipse limits

An eclipse needs a syzygy near a lunar node. The tool measures the Moon's distance in longitude from the nearer node at the exact new/full moon and applies the NASA/Espenak ecliptic limits: solar — certain under 15.39°, impossible over 18.59°; lunar (any type, penumbral included) — certain under 15.3°, impossible over 17.1°; the classical umbral lunar limits are 9°30′ / 12°15′. Between the bounds lies the uncertain band: “an eclipse is possible” — exactly the prediction the Babylonian 223-month cycle delivered. As ground truth, the bundled engine's global searches (SearchGlobalSolarEclipse / SearchLunarEclipse) report what actually happened at that syzygy. All criteria are global: they say an eclipse occurred or was possible somewhere on Earth — local visibility would need Besselian elements and is never claimed here.

How far back the numbers can be trusted

Every computation is range-guarded by the calendar module's era-accuracy tiers: casting-grade (1600–2200, arcminute confidence), study-grade (500–1600 and 2200–3000, sign and degree reliable), illustrative (before 500 CE, whole degrees only — ΔT is uncertain by minutes of time), and refuse outside −1999…3000, where the tool declines to compute at all.
