How this works

The Orchestrator adds no new astrology. It hands Claude the same engine functions the rest of the site runs — as a set of callable tools — and lets the model decide which to run for your question:

  • Your key, your browser. The request goes straight from this page to Anthropic's endpoint with the key you paste (Claude only — the free Groq tier can't drive tools). Nothing is proxied; a key you saved on the Master Tool is reused here (same private store on your device).
  • Plan → compute → explain. Claude first restates the question and plans which engines answer it (calling listCapabilities — the registry index — when unsure), then calls the tools to compute the real figures (never inventing a number), then translates each result: the book meaning, then the plain-words reading, grounded on the tool output.
  • The tools. fullChart (the unified Western reading + the Vedic mirror), vedicChart, transitHits, synastryPair, annualChart (Tājika varṣaphala), prasnaNow & muhurtaDay (the Indian horary & electional clocks), momentScan (every rulebook, one timeline), greatConjunctions, timelords (progressions · firdaria · zodiacal releasing), the oracles (castRunes, drawTarot, castIChing, castGeomancy) and defineTerm — a dozen engines and more, each result condensed and cited, carrying the honest caveat.
  • Auditable. Every engine Claude runs shows in the log as a collapsible ⚙ toolName(args) line with the computed result, so you can check every figure the reading rests on. Randomness for the oracles is thrown by your browser (crypto), never by the model.
  • New engines join automatically. When a future tool is added to the registry and the tool surface, the Orchestrator discovers and uses it with nothing else to change — the same extension pattern the whole site is built on.

Surface composed: core/llm-context.js (toAnthropicTools · runTool · buildOrchestratorPrompt) over every engine module; transport app/llm-core.js (the Anthropic Messages API). Each engine carries its own citations on its own page.

A strong, honest caveat None of this predicts or measures anything in the real world. Where two traditions agree, that agreement means nothing more than a coincidence of conventions; where they disagree (the usual case), that is by design, not a contradiction to resolve. The Orchestrator's worth is as a structured mirror for study and reflection — described, never prescribed. See Sources & Science.
