These are factual observations about other software, surveyed on the dates shown. Prices and features change; every row carries its source and survey date. Most of these products are excellent at what they do — the last column of every card says what they do better than us. We surveyed ~30 products in 2026-07.

The bar this page has to clear This is the most dangerous page on the site: one puffed claim and it fails the site's own honesty standard. So the rule is strict — every cell is a checkable fact about software, not a value judgement; every competitor is dated and sourced; every novelty claim can be falsified in under a minute; and the "what others do better" column is mandatory and non-empty for every product. 1 · Method & honesty note

Each product was checked with live web search on the survey date recorded in its card (the page-level stamp is 2026-07). We asked three questions of every product, and recorded software facts — never a judgement of whether its astrology "works":

Explains? Does it teach the method, or only output results?
Cites? Does it name primary sources or editions for its rules?
Validity? Does it state anywhere that astrology or divination has no demonstrated empirical validity?

In the matrix each cell is one of four states: ● full   ◐ partial   ○ absent   ? unverified. The last is our CONTESTED mechanism carried across: when a fact could not be confirmed from a public source we show ? rather than guess.

Found an error? The sources are one click away Facts go stale — prices and features will be wrong within a year, and that is disclosed here, not hidden. Every row links its source; if a fact is out of date or wrong, tell us and we will treat it like a CONTESTED flag: correct the record and keep the old value in a note if it changed materially. Two claims below (the cross-tradition atlas, the hand-calculation checker) are the perishable ones — a single new product could falsify them, which is why they are phrased "we found none in our 2026-07 survey."
