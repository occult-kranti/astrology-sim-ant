This curriculum teaches you to compute and read a historical tradition — what astrologers and magicians believed and how they reasoned. The astronomy and the arithmetic are real and reproducible; the interpretations are the tradition's, described, never prescribed. Magical and ritual material is recorded as history only, never as instruction.
✶ Why the math comes first In 1570 John Dee prefaced the first English Euclid with a case that the whole art rests on number. He filed astrology itself under the mathematical arts:
"Astrologie is an Arte Mathematicall, which reasonably demonstrateth the operations and effectes of the naturall beames of light, and secrete influence of the Sterres and Planets…"
"O comfortable allurement, O ravishing perswasion, to deale with a Science whose Subiect is so Auncient, so pure, so excellent, so surmounting all creatures…"
— John Dee, Mathematicall Praeface to Billingsley's Euclid (London, 1570); public domain, Project Gutenberg #22062.

Dee's frame is our starting point with one honest correction: the site keeps only the astronomy and arithmetic as demonstrably real. The step from a true map of the sky to a claim about a life is the interpretive move, and it has never been shown to work. Learn the mathematics for the craft and the history; keep the honesty about what it can and cannot tell you.

2.1

The celestial sphere, the ecliptic & the zodiac

mathneeds: 1.2~20 min

A chart is a map of the sky for one moment seen from one place. The Sun's yearly path is the ecliptic, and the band of sky along it is divided into the twelve signs. Learn why Lilly's tropical zodiac is pinned to the seasons, not the stars.

the arithmetic The ecliptic is cut into 12 signs of 30° each: 12 × 30° = 360°. In the tropical zodiac, 0° Aries is fixed to the spring equinox — the point the Sun crosses going north — not to any star.

2.2

Coordinates & Local Sidereal Time

mathneeds: 2.1~30 min

The single hardest number in casting a chart is the Local Sidereal Time — the sky's own clock at your longitude, which fixes what degree is rising. Work it by hand at Station 2 of the hand-calc tool, which checks your arithmetic against the exact engine and shows the delta.

the arithmetic LST = ST@prior-midnight + UT-interval + acceleration + longitude ÷ 15 (East adds, West subtracts), reduced below 24h. Sidereal time gains ≈ 3m 56.6s on the Sun each day; the hand-book rule is 10 s per hour of interval — (h × 10 + m ÷ 6) seconds — beside the exact mean rate 9.8565 s/hour.

3.3

Aspects & reception

mathneeds: 3.1~25 min

An aspect is a meaningful angle by which two planets "see" and act on one another; whether it is applying (coming about) or separating (past) is the heart of horary timing. Reception — a planet sitting in another's dignity — softens even a hard aspect.

the arithmetic Five Ptolemaic angles: conjunction 0°, sextile 60°, square 90°, trine 120°, opposition 180°. Orbs belong to the planets (Saturn 9°, Jupiter 9°, Mars 7°, Sun 15°, Venus 7°, Mercury 7°, Moon 12°); the two half-orbs (moieties) add, and bodies are in aspect when |separation − angle| ≤ moiety(A) + moiety(B).

M4 · The math II

4.1

Ephemerides & interpolation

mathneeds: 2.2~20 min

An ephemeris prints each planet's place at midnight; to find it at the birth moment you interpolate between the day's endpoints. This is the "simple proportion" every hand-cast chart needs.

the arithmetic position = midnight-position + daily-motion × (interval ÷ 24h). A planet at 10° moving 1°/day, six hours after midnight, sits at 10° + 1° × 6/24 = 10°15′.

4.2

Proportional logarithms

mathneeds: 4.1~25 min

The classical labour-saver: the proportional logarithm turns the interpolation multiplication into the addition of two table look-ups. It is a computational shortcut, not an accuracy gain — both routes give the same answer, and both inherit the same one-day error (negligible for the Sun, ~2.7′ for the fast Moon).

the arithmetic plog(x) = log₁₀(1440 ÷ x-in-minutes). Because motion = daily-motion × interval ÷ 1440 min, plog(daily-motion) + plog(interval) = plog(the motion). DeVore's table: plog 14°27′ = .22034, plog 7h35m = .50035, sum .72069 → 4°34′.

4.3

House cusps by trigonometry

mathneeds: 2.2~25 min

The Ascendant and Midheaven — and every cusp — come from spherical trigonometry of your sidereal time, latitude and the ecliptic's tilt. By hand you read a Book of Tables and interpolate twice; the Ascendant needs the latitude step, the Midheaven does not.

the arithmetic The Book of Tables steps one row per 4 minutes of sidereal time — exactly 1° of right ascension of the Midheaven — down the rows, and one column per degree of latitude. You interpolate bilinearly between the four surrounding cells; the double-interpolated Ascendant lands ~0.05′ from the engine's exact value.

4.4

ΔT, calendars & era accuracy

mathneeds: 2.2~20 min

To cast a historical moment you need three corrections the software handles quietly: the clock/Earth mismatch ΔT, the Julian→Gregorian calendar shift before 1582, and the slow drift of the equinox by precession. The Cycles engine tags each historical moment with an accuracy tier for exactly this reason.

the arithmetic Universal Time and the uniform Terrestrial Time the ephemeris runs on differ by ΔT (≈ +69 s in the 2020s, growing to hours millennia ago). Dates before 1582 need the Julian→Gregorian shift, and the equinox itself creeps westward by precession ≈ 50.29″ per year (a full 360° circuit in ~25,800 years).

5.1

Essential dignity & the almuten

mathneeds: 3.1~25 min

Essential dignity scores how "at home" a planet is by the sign and degree it occupies. The almuten of a degree is its rightful lord — the planet with the most dignity there — which can differ from the simple domicile ruler.

the arithmetic Five rulerships score by sign/degree: domicile +5, exaltation +4, triplicity +3, term +2, face +1; against them detriment −5, fall −4, and (dignified nowhere it stands) peregrine −5. The almuten is the planet with the highest sum of the positive points at that degree.

6.1

Tropical vs sidereal — the ayanāṁśa arithmetic

mathneeds: 2.1, 4.4~20 min

The single difference that splits the two zodiacs: Lilly measures from the equinox (tropical), Jyotiṣa from the fixed stars (sidereal). The gap between them is the ayanāṁśa, and it is pure subtraction.

the arithmetic sidereal longitude = tropical longitude − ayanāṁśa. The ayanāṁśa grows with precession ≈ 50.29″ per year, so the two zodiacs drift about 1° apart every ~72 years — roughly 24° today (Lahiri). A planet at 24° Taurus tropical sits near 0° Taurus sidereally.

7.2

The magic squares (kameas)

mathneeds: 7.1~25 min

The seven planetary kameas (Agrippa II.22) are genuine magic squares — every row, column and diagonal summing to the same constant — and the site verifies each grid live. Their Indian sibling, the navagraha yantras, uses the same mathematics with the opposite planetary logic.

the arithmetic A magic square of order n on the numbers 1…n² has magic constant M = n(n² + 1) ÷ 2: the 3×3 (Saturn / Sūrya) sums to 15, the 4×4 (Jupiter) to 34, the 5×5 (Mars) to 65, up to the 9×9 Moon at 369. The nine navagraha squares together total 729.

7.3

Kabbalah & gematria

mathneeds: 1.1~25 min

The Tree of Life (Kircher layout, 1652–54) joins ten sephiroth by twenty-two paths, one per Hebrew letter. Its arithmetic engine is gematria: every letter carries a number, so every word carries a sum. The explorer computes it live over the 22-letter alphabet.

the arithmetic Gematria is pure addition: each letter has a value (aleph 1, bet 2, … yod 10, … qof 100, resh 200, shin 300, tav 400), and a word's value is their sum. The mispar-gadol variant gives the five final forms their own higher values (500–900).

the arithmetic The I Ching's three fair coins (tails 2, heads 3) give per line: P(6 old yin) = P(9 old yang) = 1/8, P(7) = P(8) = 3/8, so any line moves with probability 1/4.
