Lilly's natal method, in order

1 · Rectification

Correcting an uncertain birth time. Lilly gives the Trutine of Hermes and Ptolemy's Animodar, but trusts most the rectification by accidents — fitting the chart to known events of the life.

2 · The Hyleg (giver of life)

The “prorogator of life”: the Sun by day or Moon by night, if in a hylegiacal place (1st, 7th, 9th, 10th or 11th); otherwise the Ascendant. The Anareta is the planet that, by direction, threatens it.

3 · The Alcocoden (giver of years)

The planet with most dignity over the hyleg's place; by its greater, mean or least years (tabulated in Book I) it indicates the possible length of life. Lilly leans far more on directing the hyleg than on a literal year-count.

4 · The Lord of the Geniture

The planet with most essential and accidental dignity, best placed and most elevated — “the most powerful and predominant” in the figure, colouring the whole life. The calculator finds it for you.

5 · Temperament & manners

The humoral balance (choleric / melancholic / sanguine / phlegmatic) from the Ascendant, its lord, the Moon, the season and the Lord of the Geniture; and the “manners” or cast of mind, chiefly from Mercury, the Moon, and the rising sign.

6 · The twelve houses of life

Then the particular fortunes — body and stature (1st), wealth (2nd), brethren (3rd), parents (4th), children (5th), and so on through the figure, each judged by its house, lord and occupants.

Prediction — how Lilly times events

Primary directions

The bulk of Book III: directing the hyleg and angles to their promittors. For the measure of time Lilly uses the Naibod rate (≈0°59′ per year of life), not a flat one degree.

Profections

The Ascendant advanced one whole sign per year of life; the lord of the new sign becomes the Lord of the Year. Lilly uses annual profections only, to time when a direction takes effect.

Revolutions (solar returns)

The chart for the Sun's return to its exact natal degree each year, read together with the directions and profections — never alone.

A point of fidelity Some traditions add firdaria (Persian time-lord periods) to this list, but they appear nowhere in Christian Astrology — a full-text search returns none. Lilly's predictive triad is directions + profections + revolutions (with transits). We follow what Lilly actually wrote.
And the honest caveat Directions and returns are precise astronomy; the claim that they predict a life is not supported by evidence. The “time-twins” study followed 2,000+ people born within days of one another and found no shared destinies — see About & Sources.
