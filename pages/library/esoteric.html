Divination & esoteric systems — the practitioners

The people behind the Workbench's oracle wings, grouped by practice: tarot, geomancy, I Ching, runes, Kabbalah, alchemy, and Jung & divination. Each practice pairs a history shelf — the scholars who show what a system actually was — with a practice shelf of the teachers who read it today, and quarantines the famous invented systems as clearly-labelled exhibits.

Honest note The methods are catalogued as taught, in reportive voice — described, never prescribed, with no validity claimed. Belief context (Druid, Heathen, Thelemic, Jungian) is flagged (⚑), and cautionary exhibits of pure invention — Ralph Blum's blank rune, Les Cross's Astrogem, the Egyptian-origin tarot myth — are labelled as exhibits of how new systems get made, not as tradition.
