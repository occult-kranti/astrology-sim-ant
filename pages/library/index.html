The Practitioners' Library

Every calculator on this site reproduces a method that real people wrote down. This wing is the deep-dive catalog of those people and their books: the canon sources, the respected modern teachers, the genuine lesser-known voices, and the academics who study the whole thing — across Western traditional astrology, the Indian systems, and the divination and esoteric arts. Each entry names who they are, their verified works, and — where a named book teaches one — the step-by-step method as its author teaches it.

Honest note — please read These are the people and the books; the methods are theirs, described as historical and contemporary practice — never prescribed, with no claim of demonstrated validity. Where a figure personally asserts that a technique works, or works from a religious/esoteric belief frame, that is flagged (⚑) on their card. Every record cites where the person↔work pairing was verified.

The four tiers

The tiers grade practitioner-recognition — how a working community places a figure — not scholarly weight or truth. A towering scholar can read as "niche" to practitioners, and a self-taught teacher as "respected"; the labels track visibility inside a craft, and the honest frame is the same across all four.

Canon

The historical sources — the foundational authors and texts a practice is built on, in their standard modern editions and translations.

Respected modern

Widely-recognized contemporary teachers — the figures a practitioner community reads, cites and trains under today.

Niche

Genuine lesser-known practitioner voices — real, verifiable teachers who are simply less visible. Practitioner-recognition, not scholarly weight: "niche" never means minor.

Academic

Scholarship, not practice — historians and philologists who study a system. They describe the art; they do not prescribe it.

On the vocabulary "Niche" means lesser-known to a working community, never minor: Michael Dummett — the pre-eminent historian of tarot — sits on the scholarship shelf that most card-readers never open, and Stuart Kaplan, who published the Rider–Waite–Smith deck for fifty years, is a central figure of the tarot world however a practitioner tier reads. Each record carries a note where the label needs that context.

How this catalog was built

Method — the fabrication check A two-pass, adversarial process (2026-07-16). First, an expert catalog of each domain's practitioners, works and step-by-step methods was compiled from named published sources only. Second, an independent reviewer re-checked every person↔work pairing by live web search against publisher/bookseller and ISBN records and the figures' own sites, hunting specifically for invented people or books. Every confirmed correction was applied — titles, years, publishers, translators, tiers and framing — anything refuted or unverifiable was removed (for example a "Bruce Robertson" with no traceable works), and each method is catalogued as its author teaches it.
