The Indian systems (Jyotiṣa) — the practitioners

The people behind the Workbench's Jyotiṣa, praśna, muhūrta and tājika tools, grouped by practice: Parāśarī natal, Jaimini, the nakṣatras, KP, tājika varṣaphala, muhūrta, praśna and nāḍī — followed by three scholarship shelves (history of jyotiṣa, rasaśāstra & alchemy, and yoga) whose academics study, rather than practise, the tradition.

Honest note The methods are catalogued as taught or claimed — described, never prescribed, with no validity claimed. Academic records are marked scholarship, not practice; remedial and mantra practices are catalogued as described tradition. Every person↔work pairing was verified, and two title-slips were corrected before encoding (K. N. Rao's book is Learn Hindu Astrology Easily; aṣṭakavarga is anchored on C. S. Patel, not V. P. Goel).
