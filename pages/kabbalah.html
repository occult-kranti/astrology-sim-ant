Kabbalah — the Tree of Life explorer & gematria

The kabbalistic Tree of Life as Athanasius Kircher drew it (Oedipus Aegyptiacus, 1652–54) and the Golden Dawn adopted it: ten sephiroth on three pillars, joined by twenty-two paths, one for each Hebrew letter. Click any sphere or path to open its record — sphere, pillar, planet (and its kamea), Hebrew letter, Sefer Yetzirah letter-class, Golden Dawn tarot trump — and use the gematria calculator below to sum any Hebrew word the way the tradition did.

Honest note — please read Everything on this page is a layered historical construction of no demonstrated validity, and each layer is dated: the Sefer Yetzirah (dating contested — mainstream c. 2nd–6th c. CE, proposals 2nd–9th) speaks of ten sefirot belimah as abstract numbers only; the named emanations are medieval Kabbalah (Bahir c. 1176, Zohar c. 1286); the earliest printed tree (1516) had only 17 paths; the 22-path layout here is Kircher's (1652–54); the tarot–letter linkage begins with Lévi (1854–56, with a different scheme); the Golden Dawn table is c. 1887–1900; Crowley edited it in 1904 and 1944. The gematria arithmetic is exact and checkable; what the tradition read into equal sums is symbolism, recorded for study — described, never prescribed. Contested values are flagged where they occur and never resolved.

How it works — what is computed here

Gematria is exact arithmetic over a fixed table: each Hebrew letter carries a standard value (mispar hechrachi: Aleph 1 … Yod 10, Kaph 20 … Qoph 100, Resh 200, Shin 300, Tau 400) and a word's value is the sum — gematria('אחד') walks the string, looks each codepoint up, and adds. The mispar-gadol toggle switches the five final forms (ך ם ן ף ץ) to their variant values 500–900; standard counting values finals the same as their medials, so the variant is flagged, never the default. Non-Hebrew characters are ignored. The engine-test proves the four classical examples (13 / 13 / 18 / 26) on every run.

The Tree is a data table, not a computation: 10 sephiroth records (pillar, sphere, planet where the tradition assigned one) and 22 path records (letter, Sefer Yetzirah class, Golden Dawn trump), drawn as SVG from normalized Kircher-layout coordinates. pathBetween(a,b) is a lookup in that table — e.g. pathBetween(1,6) → path 13, Gimel, the High Priestess. The seven planetary sephiroth join the seven kameas (Agrippa II.22) by planet name, and every path's trump joins the tarot deck by id — the engine-test asserts both joins hold, letter for letter.

What is NOT computed: any meaning. The letter-to-planet assignment for the seven doubles is genuinely contested between recensions (the table above shows all four attested columns), the Golden Dawn's own order matches no manuscript, and none of the correspondences has demonstrated validity. The site displays the GD standard because the tree shown is the GD's — a choice of what to describe, not a resolution.

A strong, honest caveat No sephira, path, letter-value or correspondence on this page describes the real world. The Tree of Life is a symbolic diagram assembled over roughly seventeen centuries by authors who never met, frequently contradicted one another (the recension table above is the proof), and whose systems have no demonstrated validity. The arithmetic is real; the meanings are the tradition's, reproduced faithfully for study — never a claim, a prediction, or a practice to follow. See Sources & Science.
