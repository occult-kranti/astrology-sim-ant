Tarot — the spread, laid & read

The Tarot's 78 cards — 22 Major Arcana and the four suits of the Minor Arcana (Wands/Fire, Cups/Water, Swords/Air, Pentacles/Earth) — read in the Rider–Waite–Smith and Golden Dawn tradition. Choose a spread, shuffle & draw (with reversals if you like), and the engine lays each card in its position, weighs the elemental dignities between neighbours, and shows the spread's balance — the weight of the Major Arcana, the leading suit, the reversals.

Honest note — please read Tarot cartomancy is a historical divinatory art of no demonstrated validity. The deck's correspondences (Hebrew letters, decans, elements) are a late-Victorian Golden Dawn / Waite–Smith synthesis (c. 1888–1911) — a symbolic grammar, not a fact about the world. The cards are shuffled by your browser's random generator; the reading is the tradition's, reproduced for study — never a prediction or advice.
