Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa — Three Books of Occult Philosophy

Agrippa's De occulta philosophia is the single largest source behind the site's Picatrix wing: its planetary squares (II.22), decan images (II.37), mansion images (II.46), Behenian stars (II.47) and geomantic figures (II.48) are exactly what the live engines compute — so the chapter map below links each range to the tool that reproduces it. The 1533 Latin and 1651 English are public domain. The famous Fourth Book is not by Agrippa — denounced as spurious by his own student Weyer c. 1563 — and is kept apart, flagged, as the wing's worked example of why provenance matters.
