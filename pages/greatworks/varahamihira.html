Varāhamihira — Bṛhajjātaka

Varāhamihira's 6th-century natal manual is the classical spine the site's Vedic (sidereal) reading reproduces: twenty-eight adhyāyas of signs, houses, planetary natures, yogas, longevity arithmetic and house-by-house delineation, each pointing at a Workbench engine that computes it — the Vedic wing for the vargas and aṣṭakavarga, the yoga detector for the rāja/nābhasa/candra yogas, praśna for the query-moment chapter. This is the expansion's licence flagship: the Bṛhajjātaka survives in two independently verified US-public-domain English translations (Iyer 1885, Sastri 1929). Its predictive methods are catalogued as the text's own historical method — no demonstrated predictive validity, never endorsed; the caste- and gender-frame verses are historicised as a 6th-century worldview, not endorsed.

Two clean public-domain translations — the licence flagship Almost alone among classical Indian authors, Varāhamihira's natal masterwork has two verified US-PD English translations: N. Chidambaram Iyer (also transliterated N. C. Aiyar), Madras 1885 (pre-1929), and V. Subrahmanya Sastri, Mysore Government Branch Press, 1929 (US PD since 1 Jan 2025 under the 95-year rule). Short quotation is permitted, always edition-cited — but only from those two editions: the archive.org scan labelled "2nd ed." is a verified 1996 reprint and is not public domain. Modern editions (Ramakrishna Bhat, B. Suryanarain Rao) are cite-only.
