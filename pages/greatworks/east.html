The Great Works — Eastern masters

The Eastern shelf of the study wing: three authors whose books stand behind the site's Indian tools, read as historical documents and mapped chapter-by-chapter onto the engines that compute what they describe. Varāhamihira — the 6th-century Ujjain astronomer whose Bṛhajjātaka the Vedic wing reproduces; Swami Vivekananda — whose Raja Yoga (1896) is his rendering of the Yoga Sūtras; and Paramahansa Yogananda — whose Autobiography of a Yogi (1946) is the wing's honest-framing stress-test. Described never prescribed; every claim cited; miracle and efficacy claims reported as the book's claims, never endorsed; contested material flagged and left both ways.
