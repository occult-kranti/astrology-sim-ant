The Hermetica — the philosophical dialogues of "Hermes"

The Greco-Egyptian wisdom dialogues attributed to Hermes Trismegistus — read in the public-domain Mead translation (1906) and mapped onto the site's engines. The Corpus Hermeticum runs I–XIV then XVI–XVIII: there is no treatise XV, and the gap is shown here, never renumbered. The Asclepius carries the god-making rite that is the direct ancestor of the talisman doctrine; the Stobaean decan excerpt is the ancestor of the 36 faces; the Emerald Tablet (Arabic-first, not part of the Corpus) closes the path in Newton's own English.
