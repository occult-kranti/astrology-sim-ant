John Dee — the mathematician among the angels

Elizabeth I's mathematician wrote the enigmatic Monas glyph, defended English mathematics in the Praeface to Euclid — where he defines Astrologie as an "Arte Mathematicall" — and, with the scryer Edward Kelley, recorded the angelic conversations that produced the Enochian system. The diaries are records of what Dee believed was happening: Casaubon's hostile 1659 frame and Laycock's later verdict (the "language" as glossolalia) are both presented, and the site adds no third claim. The dated séances are timestamps the historical-calculation layer can cast.
