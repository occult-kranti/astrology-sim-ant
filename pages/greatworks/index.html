The Great Works — author → book → chapter study guides

The books behind the Workbench's esoteric wings, read as historical documents and mapped chapter-by-chapter onto the tools that compute what they describe. Nine authors — Manly P. Hall, the Hermetica, Aleister Crowley, John Dee, Agrippa, and the kindred voices Lévi, Ficino, Iamblichus and Regardie — each with its works, its ordered study path, and its "magic" catalogued as a museum piece. The framing is the site's: described never prescribed, every claim cited, contested and spurious material flagged and never resolved.

Eastern masters

The same treatment, applied to the authors behind the site's Indian tools. Varāhamihira's natal manual is what the Vedic (sidereal) engine reproduces; Vivekananda's Raja Yoga (1896) is his rendering of the Yoga Sūtras; Yogananda's Autobiography of a Yogi (1946 first edition) is the wing's honest-framing stress-test. Miracle and efficacy claims are reported as the book's claims, never endorsed.
