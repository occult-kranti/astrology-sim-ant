Bhāva delineations — the two-witness browser

The largest body of Jyotiṣa doctrine is the graha-in-bhāva literature: what a planet is held to signify in each of the twelve houses. This browser holds 108 cited records — the nine grahas across the twelve bhāvas — with two witnesses side by side and never merged: Mantreśvara's Phaladīpikā (ch. 8) and Kalyāṇavarman's Sārāvalī (ch. 30). Where they agree it is marked; where they genuinely clash, the clash is shown as a clash, not smoothed into one "meaning". Pick any cell of the 12×9 grid, or read your own chart's nine placements.

Honest note — please read Every delineation below is a historical doctrinal claim with no demonstrated validity. The summaries are original prose reporting exactly what the cited verse says — including its harsh, deterministic judgments, reproduced unsoftened for fidelity, describing nothing about any actual person. The two texts are kept apart on purpose: their disagreement is a feature of the tradition to be shown, not a problem to be resolved. Sārāvalī has no Rāhu/Ketu house verses; those records are single-witness with the absence noted honestly. See Sources & Science.

How to read the two witnesses

Each record shows both texts in full, in their own column, with the exact verse locus. When they broadly concur the record is badged agree; when they overlap but diverge, partial; when they truly conflict — the eleventh-house Sun glowing as a sorrowless king in Phaladīpikā and soured into an affectionless servant in Sārāvalī, for instance — the record is badged contradiction and a callout states the clash. Nothing is merged into a single verdict, because the tradition itself holds both.
