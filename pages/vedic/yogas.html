Vedic yogas — the detector

A yoga is a named planetary combination the Jyotiṣa texts hold to carry a fixed effect. This tool reads a birth moment and evaluates 36 cited yoga rules — the five pañca-mahāpuruṣa, the solar and lunar combinations, the dhana (wealth) and rāja (sovereignty) sets, the Vipreet trio, Nīca-bhaṅga and the rest — predicate by predicate: each condition ticked ✓ or ✗ with its detail, the fruit the text claims, and its verse. Where the tradition genuinely disagrees, the rule is flagged CONTESTED and every position shown verbatim — a contested yoga is never collapsed to a single yes/no.

How the detector works

Each yoga is stored as data, not code: a list of conditions in a small predicate grammar (placement in a house-group from a reference point, lordship of a house, sambandha between two selectors, a dignity/affliction constraint, or the absence of a graha), plus the fruit the text claims and its citation. The engine evaluates the conditions against the sidereal whole-sign chart and reports every condition's outcome — so the reasoning is always visible, never a black box.

Contested rules are never a boolean. Where the classical editions disagree — the Gaja-Kesarī strength conditions, the Kemadruma cancellations, the Vipreet "must not associate" rider, the three Nīca-bhaṅga condition sets, the kendrādhipati-doṣa dispute — the rule carries a CONTESTED panel that prints each position verbatim and resolves nothing. The reference point (Lagna vs Moon) for the mahāpuruṣa kendra reckoning is exposed as a setting, defaulting to the Lagna as BPHS defines it.

Teaching order after a 132-lesson classroom playlist (a modern pedagogical witness, not an authority); all rules cited to the editions in the Indian library. The detector describes what the tradition holds; it predicts nothing.

A strong, honest caveat None of this predicts or determines anything about anyone. The yoga rules are a faithful, cited reconstruction of how the Jyotiṣa literature reasoned about planetary combinations — a historical symbolic grammar with its genuine disagreements preserved rather than smoothed over. Interesting history, not guidance. See Sources & Science.
