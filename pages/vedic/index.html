Jagannath Hora — the Vedic (sidereal) reading

Astronomy — verified Doctrine — historical Predictive validity — none demonstrated

A second, independent system. Where the rest of this site computes William Lilly's Western, tropical astrology, this page computes the Vedic (Jyotiṣa) horoscope as Jagannath Hora does — the sidereal zodiac (tropical − Lahiri ayanāṁśa), whole-sign houses, the 27 nakṣatras, the Vimśottarī daśā, the Pañcāṅga, the divisional charts (vargas), the Aṣṭakavarga, and a partial Ṣaḍbala. The astronomy is the same verified engine; only the zodiac, houses and methods differ.

The two systems disagree by design. The same planet sits ~24° earlier in the sidereal zodiac, so it often lands in a different sign and house than the Western chart. That is expected — see the worked example below, and toggle 🕉 Jagannath Hora — Vedic view on any calculator to compare them side by side.

The reading below now also surfaces the pañcadhā-maitrī friendship matrix, the graha-dṛṣṭi aspect grid and per-graha combustion (asta) flags.

Worked example — reading a Vedic chart

Take the default chart above (15 May 1990, 12:00, London). Here is how the tradition reads it, step by step — every figure recomputes live above, so you can change the birth data and watch the reasoning move:

  1. Sidereal shift. The Lahiri ayanāṁśa for 1990 is ≈23.7°, so every tropical longitude is pulled back ~24°. The Sun, ~24° Taurus in the Western chart, becomes 0°41′ Taurus sidereally — a different degree, and often a different sign than the Western reading.
  2. Lagna & houses. The sidereal Ascendant is Leo, so Leo is the 1st bhāva and the houses run whole-sign from there. The Sun in Taurus therefore sits in the 10th (career, status) — the kāraka of the soul on the house of action.
  3. Dignities. Venus is exalted in Pisces (8th) and Saturn is in its own sign Capricorn (6th): two strong grahas. A planet's strength is read first by sign (exalted / own / mūlatrikoṇa / debilitated), then confirmed by the divisional charts and Ṣaḍbala.
  4. Nakṣatra & daśā. The Moon sits in Uttara Āṣāḍhā, ruled by the Sun, so life opens in a Sun mahā-daśā; by 2026 the chart is deep in the Rāhu mahā-daśā. The Vimśottarī daśā is the spine of Vedic timing — the running mahā/antar lord colours the period.
  5. Pañcāṅga. The five limbs of the day (tithi, vāra, nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa) frame the muhūrta — the Vedic counterpart of Lilly's election: which moment is fit for which act.
  6. Aṣṭakavarga. The Sarvāṣṭakavarga distributes 337 points across the 12 signs (average 28); a sign with >28 bindus is a strong field for the planets transiting it — a quick, numeric strength map of the whole chart.
