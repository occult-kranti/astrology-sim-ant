The texts & the scholarship

The Indian ritual-magic tradition is a dated chain of texts — from the Atharvaveda's Vedic charms through the medieval tantric compendia to Mahīdhara's late systematization of 1588 — read through a century of critical scholarship. This page lists every source with its editions and its attribution flags, then states plainly what this wing verified — including the former direction/deity gap, now closed.

The modern scholarship

This wing follows the scholarship, and quotes primary texts only in their public-domain translations to stay licence-clean. The tables trace, source by source, to:

  • Goudriaan, Māyā Divine and Human (Motilal Banarsidass 1978) — the ṣaṭkarman chapter; the standard treatment.
  • Bühnemann, "The Six Rites of Magic," in D. G. White (ed.), Tantra in Practice (Princeton UP 2000, pp. 447-462), and Iconography of Hindu Tantric Deities (Groningen 2000) — the Mantramahodadhi 25 apparatus, the "nineteen items," and the deity/colour/direction/season tables.
  • Türstig, "The Indian Sorcery Called Abhicāra" (WZKS 29 (1985): 69-117 — ⚑ volume number flagged; an OCR showed "24") — the direction/deity chart to cross-check.
  • White, Kiss of the Yoginī; Sinister Yogis — the wider tantric-magic context.
  • Ullrey, Grim Grimoires: Pragmatic Ritual in the Magic Tantras (UCSB diss. 2016; eScholarship qt4vt6f325) — every Uḍḍīśatantra / Bhairavapadmāvatīkalpa quote on this site traces through it.
  • Bloomfield (SBE 42, 1897) and Whitney-Lanman (HOS 7-8, 1905), with Caland, Altindisches Zauberritual (1900) for the Kauśika applications — all public domain, and the only translations quoted at length.

Coverage & limits — what we verified, and the one gap

Verified & safe to encode The season/time schemes (both, flagged as the texts' own conflict), the colours, the mat-symbols in both text traditions, the mudrā shapes, the rosary materials, the finger-combinations, the left-hand vaśya rule, the dark-fortnight māraṇa timing, the 19-item Mantramahodadhi apparatus schema, the Atharvaveda hymn corpus with its Kauśika applications, and the text datings — all were adversarially re-verified against the primary and scholarly sources in full text (2026-07-16), with every correction applied in-data (e.g. the day-part scheme is the Bhairavapadmāvatīkalpa not the Uḍḍīśatantra; ākṛṣṭi's colour is udayārka; the Book-7 hymn numbers are Whitney/Bloomfield, not the vulgate; the Tantrasārasaṃgraha's "destruction" is utsādana).
Former data gap — now verified (2026-07-16) The per-act direction (dik) and presiding deity rows of the Śāradātilaka 23 / Mantramahodadhi 25 chart were the wing's one open gap; an accuracy-check pass has now confirmed every row — both act→direction and act→deity — verbatim against two independent full-text sources: Bühnemann's Tantra in Practice chapter (pp. 448-449, Table 26.1 goddess+colour and Table 26.2 season+direction, the Mantramahodadhi 25 scheme) and Ullrey's independent translation of a different base text, the Uḍḍīśatantra 1.13-15. In śānti→māraṇa order the verified rows are: deities Rati · Vāṇī(Sarasvatī) · Ramā · Jyeṣṭhā · Durgā · Kālī; directions faced NE · N · E · SW · NW · SE — south and west omitted ("the two remaining cardinal and the four intermediate directions are of significance", Bühnemann p. 449); with the MMU goddess-colour scheme white · red · yellow · variegated · dark · gray. This is the direction the practitioner faces (MMU 25.9cd-10ab); the separate fire-pit orientation (MMU 25.60-61ab) differs for two of the rites. Bühnemann pp. 448-449 (MMU 25.6-10ab, Tables 26.1-26.2); Ullrey, Uḍḍīśatantra 1.13-15 (diss. pp. 649-650).
