The Roadmap — planned tools & wings

Where the Workbench is going next. Every item below is a plan, not a promise — a titled stub with a two-line brief, a planned marker, and a note of the engines it will reuse rather than re-implement. The sections already exist so future rounds simply fill them; nothing here links to a page that isn't built yet.

Read this first — the same honest footing A roadmap is not a claim of validity. Everything planned here inherits the site's rule: the mathematics is real and verifiable; the interpretations are historical doctrine, described for study, never prescribed. Items marked with a question mark are candidates — they ship only if they can be framed honestly as history. See Sources & Science for the standing assessment.

Already shipped and looking for what exists today? The All Tools table lists every current calculator and the Master Index maps the whole site. This page is only the future plan.

1 · Western astrology — depth

Finishing the classical apparatus of Lilly and his sources: the timing techniques, the fixed-star and lot machinery, and the last steps toward judgement that is weighted like a real astrologer's.

Directions & timing

planned

Placidian mundane primary directions

The rigorous semi-arc directions (promissor to significator through the mundane houses), replacing today's self-flagged in-zodiac Naibod approximation with casting-grade arcs.

Reuses core/directions.js · core/astro.js (oblique ascension, semi-arcs) · surfaces on book3/master.html.

planned

Decumbiture mode

The 6th-house chart of the moment of falling ill: the sixteen critical days by the Moon's motion, the crises, and the physician's significators — Lilly's medical horary, laid out as its own reader.

Reuses core/horary-judge.js · core/aspects.js · core/considerations.js (the Moon's condition).

Stars, parts & points

planned

Fixed-star heliacal phases

Brady-style risings and settings: when a star first appears before dawn or last vanishes at dusk, computed from its arcus visionis — the oldest layer of star astrology, done astronomically.

Reuses core/astro.js (rise/set, altitude) · data/behenian-stars.js (with live precession).

planned

The full Arabic-parts catalog

Beyond Fortune & Spirit: the seven Hermetic lots (Eros, Necessity, Victory, Basis, Courage, Nemesis) and the topic lots, each with a sect-aware day/night formula and its source.

Reuses core/astro.js lot() · core/reading.js (reading.lots) — the affine math already exists, half-wired.

planned

Midpoints & Uranian points

The midpoint tree and the eight hypothetical Uranian planets — included strictly as a flagged-modern 20th-century system, clearly separated from Lilly's traditional doctrine.

Reuses core/astro.js · core/aspects.js (the 90°-dial harmonics) — labelled MODERN in-data throughout.

Judgement fidelity

planned

Significator-weighted chart health

Weight each caution by whether it actually touches the querent's or quesited's significators, not a flat count — the single most-cited fidelity fix, bringing the verdict closer to Lilly's real method.

Reuses core/cautions.js · core/horary-judge.js · core/perfection.js — reweighting, not new astronomy.

planned

Antiscia woven into judgement

Treat antiscia and contra-antiscia as hidden contacts inside the perfection and aspect search, as Lilly does — today they are computed and displayed but not used in the verdict.

Reuses core/aspects.js · core/perfection.js · core/astro.js (antiscion/contraAntiscion).

2 · Vedic (Jyotiṣa) — depth

The second, independent system already computes the chart, the daśās, the vargas, the Aṣṭakavarga and the full Ṣaḍbala. These extend it toward returns, compatibility, transits and the advanced schools.

Returns & compatibility

planned

Tithi-praveśa — the monthly return

The chart cast for each return of the natal tithi (lunar day) — the Indian monthly-return technique, the sidereal cousin of the solar revolution already built for the Western side.

Reuses core/vedic.js (pañcāṅga) · core/solar-return.js (the bracket-and-bisect instant finder).

planned

Nakṣatra porutham — the match grid

The ten (or twelve) kūṭa compatibility factors scored between two charts from the Moon's nakṣatra and rāśi — described as a cultural matching custom, with every factor's rule cited.

Reuses core/vedic.js · data/vedic-data.js (nakṣatra table) — a scoring grid over two existing charts.

Transits & the advanced schools

planned

Aṣṭakavarga transit overlay

Gochara read through the bindus: overlay the current transiting grahas on the natal BAV/SAV so the strong-and-weak transit houses emerge from the bindu counts already computed.

Reuses core/vedic.js (BAV/SAV, checksum-verified) + the transits timeline pattern from transits.html.

planned

Jaimini basics

The chara kārakas (the seven significators by longitude), the Ārūḍha lagna, and chara daśā — the second great Indian school, kept clearly distinct from the Parāśarī default.

Reuses core/vedic.js · data/vedic-data.js — a longitude sort and the sign-based daśā over the existing chart.

planned

Alternate daśā systems

Yoginī (8-fold) and a Kālacakra sketch alongside the built Vimśottarī, so the daśā panel can show more than one timeline of rulership — each with its span and source flagged.

Reuses core/vedic.js (the Vimśottarī daśā scaffold) — new period tables, same balance-from-Moon machinery.

3 · The oracles & esoterica

Geomancy, tarot, the I Ching, the runes and the Kabbalah tree are built. These add further oracles (only where they can be framed honestly as history) and deepen the ones already here.

Candidate new oracles

planned

Ogham — the tree alphabet

The twenty (later twenty-five) fews cast and read, with the medieval word-oghams — presented as Irish manuscript tradition, its modern divinatory use clearly labelled as a revival.

Reuses the shared diviner pattern (a pure deal/draw core + an AI narrator), as core/runes.js already does for the Futhark.

planned

Playing-card cartomancy

The history of reading with a 52-card deck (the Lenormand and piquet traditions) plus a computable cast — the folk cousin of the tarot, sourced and dated.

Reuses core/tarot.js (the shuffle / draw / spread core) with a playing-card correspondence table.

planned

Enochian chess?

The Golden Dawn's four elemental boards — a candidate only, and only if it can be presented as cipher-and-history rather than as a working practice. May not ship.

Reuses generic board / draw utilities; framing to be settled before any build.

Deepening the built oracles

planned

The yarrow-stalk probabilities explainer

Why the ancient yarrow-stalk method and the three-coin method give different odds for the moving lines — an honest probability sidebar on the I Ching, computed and charted.

Reuses core/iching.js (the line-generation core) — an added probability model, no change to the cast.

planned

Numerology beyond gematria

The name/number reduction systems (Pythagorean, Chaldean) as a small cultural add-on to the Kabbalah gematria — flagged a weak engine fit, so it stays reference-level.

Reuses the gematria core behind kabbalah.html — letter-value maps only.

4 · Practice & platform layers

planned

planned

The practitioners' library wing

A cross-tradition reading room gathering the sourced primary texts and scholarship in one browsable place — seeded by the Hermetic library already in the chronology wing.

Reuses the chronology library shell + the citation data already carried per record across the data modules.

planned

Per-tool worked-example galleries

Every calculator gets a small gallery of famous, dated charts read end-to-end — extending the live Lilly worked-charts pattern to the nativity, election, Vedic and oracle tools.

Reuses core/chart-from-positions.js · data/worked-charts.js (the cast-a-printed-figure path built for Book II).

planned

Planetary seals & sigils

A sigil-generation layer over the existing kāmeas: trace a name or number across the magic square to draw its seal — flagged clearly as Agrippa (II.22), not strictly Picatrix.

Reuses picatrix/kameas.html (the built magic squares) · data/planetary-magic.js (the correspondences).
