Book I — An Introduction to Astrology

The “alphabet” of the art. Before any question can be judged, Lilly teaches the building blocks: the twelve signs, the seven planets, the twelve houses, the five aspects, and above all the essential dignities by which a planet's strength is judged.

Honest note Astrology has no demonstrated predictive validity and is classed by science as a pseudoscience. These fundamentals are presented as the doctrine of a historical tradition — to learn and compute — described, never prescribed. New to the words? See The Basics. (Sources & Science.)

The chapters of Book I

Lilly's first book runs to twenty-one chapters. The first six teach the mechanics of erecting a figure from an ephemeris; the remainder are the astrological alphabet:

  1. I–VI — The planets, signs and aspects in brief; the use of the ephemeris; erecting a figure; reducing the planets' motion to the hour; the twelve houses and terms of art.
  2. VII — The nature and signification of the twelve houses.
  3. VIII–XIV — The seven planets in turn: Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, the Sun, Venus, Mercury, the Moon.
  4. XV — A brief description of the shapes and colours of the planets.
  5. XVI–XVII — The twelve signs, their divisions (including the antiscia), natures, diseases, places and countries.
  6. XVIII — The essential dignities of the planets, with the great Table.
  7. XIX — Aspects, orbs, application and separation, combustion, cazimi, reception, and the table of fortitudes and debilities.
  8. XX–XXI — Considerations before judgement; what the significator, querent and quesited are.

Essential dignity — the heart of Book I

A planet is judged strong or weak first by its essential dignity — its relationship to the sign and degree it occupies. Lilly gives five dignities, with a point value each, and three debilities:

DignityPointsDebilityPoints
Domicile (own house)+5Detriment−5
Exaltation+4Fall−4
Triplicity+3Peregrine−5
Term (bound)+2
Face (decan)+1
Worked example — step by step
Take Saturn at 21° Libra in a day chart.
Is Libra Saturn's domicile? No — Libra is ruled by Venus. (0 points)
Is Saturn exalted in Libra? Yes — Saturn's exaltation is 21° Libra exactly, and we are on the very degree. +4
Does Saturn rule the triplicity? Libra is an Air sign; by day the Air triplicity is ruled by Saturn. +3
The term at 21° Libra belongs to Mercury, and the face to Jupiter — not Saturn. (0)
No detriment or fall. Net essential dignity: +7 — Saturn is very strong here. Try it yourself in the calculator.

The aspects & Lilly's orbs

Lilly uses the five Ptolemaic aspects. Crucially, he assigns orbs to the planets, not to the aspects: two planets are within orb of an aspect when their distance from exactitude is less than the sum of their two moieties (half-orbs).
