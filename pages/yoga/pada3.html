Vibhūti-pāda — Book III, the powers (55 / 56 sūtras)

The third book completes the eight limbs — dhāraṇā, dhyāna, samādhi (III.1–3) — and fuses the three into saṃyama (III.4), the instrument of every vibhūti (power) that follows: from knowledge of past and future, of the cries of all beings, of others' minds, through invisibility, foreknowledge of death, mastery of the elements and the senses, up to omniscience. Then the pivot: III.37 declares that these powers are obstacles to concentration — perfections only from the standpoint of the outgoing mind — and the book ends turning toward kaivalya. The siddhis are the text's own claims, described never prescribed.

Dual numbering — read this Book III is the one book whose sūtra count is disputed. The inclusive editions (Taimni, Satchidananda, Iyengar) count a sūtra "etena śabdādyantardhānam uktam" ("by this the disappearance of sound & the rest is explained") after the invisibility sūtra, giving 56 sūtras and the whole text 196. The Vyāsa-bhāṣya vulgate — sanskritdocuments, Woods (1914), Rama Prasada (1912), Bhoja — omits it, giving 55 and 195. Below, that record is badged as the variant, and the offset it causes (every later sūtra shifts by one) is why the two numbering schemes diverge from it onward.
