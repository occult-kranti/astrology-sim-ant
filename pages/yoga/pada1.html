Samādhi-pāda — Book I, on concentration (51 sūtras)

The first book defines the whole subject and its goal. Yoga is citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ — the restriction of the fluctuations of mind-stuff (I.2); the five fluctuations are named and analysed; the twin means are practice (abhyāsa) and dispassion (vairāgya); devotion to the Īśvara is offered as an aid; the obstacles are catalogued; and the book climbs the ladder of samāpatti (the balanced states) to seedless concentration (nirbīja-samādhi).
