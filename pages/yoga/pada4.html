Kaivalya-pāda — Book IV, isolation (34 sūtras)

The final book is the most philosophical. It opens by tracing the siddhis to five sources (birth, herbs, mantra, austerity, concentration — IV.1), then argues, against the Buddhist idealists, for the reality of the external object and the immutable seer (puruṣa) who is not the mind; it follows the mind as it "slopes toward kaivalya", reaches the dharma-megha ("rain-cloud of dharma") concentration, and defines kaivalya — Isolation — in the closing sūtra as the guṇas resolved back to their source, or the power of consciousness grounded in itself.

Bhoja's numbering Bhoja's Rājamārtaṇḍa treats the vulgate IV.16 ("na caikacittatantraṃ vastu…", the anti-idealist sūtra) as commentary rather than a sūtra, so his Book IV has 33 sūtras and his numbers run one behind the vulgate from IV.17 onward. Records below carry a Bhoja IV.n badge where his number differs, and IV.16 is flagged as absent in his edition.
