Sādhana-pāda — Book II, the means (55 sūtras)

The second book is practical. It opens with kriyā-yoga (self-discipline, self-study, devotion — II.1), diagnoses the five kleśas (afflictions) rooted in avidyā, sets out the relation of the seer and the seen, and then lists the eight limbs (aṣṭāṅga, II.29) and works through the first five: yama, niyama, āsana, prāṇāyāma, pratyāhāra. The famous posture sūtra II.46 — sthira-sukham āsanam — is here, and it names no posture at all; the named postures belong to the commentary (see the āsana honesty layer). The "grounding" effects of the yamas and niyamas (II.35–45) are the text's own claims, described not prescribed.
