Synastry — the chart-to-chart aspect grid

Synastry compares two nativities: the aspects one person's planets make to the other's, and where each person's planets fall in the other's houses. Both Catherine Urban (multi-chart "family dynamics" work) and Patrick Watson (who tutors synastry) practise it. This lays the full cross-aspect grid — person A down the side, person B across the top — with Lilly's moieties, applying/separating and the whole-sign aspect, then the house overlays both ways. The classical kernel is Ptolemy's rule that the two charts' luminaries in harmonious aspect make a lasting marriage; the full planet-by-planet grid is a modern convention, flagged as such.

Honest note — please read Synastry has no demonstrated validity. The arithmetic is real — the same aspect engine, orbs and house cusps as every other tool — but the meaning read into a cross-aspect is a symbolic convention, not a measure of any relationship. The one cited classical rule is Ptolemy, Tetrabiblos IV.5 (marriage lasting when both genitures' luminaries are in harmonious aspect, "particularly… by exchange") and Dorotheus' marriage significations (Carmen Bk II); the full grid is modern. The honest bridge is Jung's 1952 marriage experiment, which tallied cross-aspects between spouses and found nothing statistically significant — our own wing re-runs it and shows that null result. How this works

The grid — one aspect engine, reused

For every pairing of one of person A's seven planets with one of person B's, the engine calls the same aspectBetween the single-chart tools use (core/aspects.js): the angular separation is tested against the five Ptolemaic aspects, "in orb" when it is within the sum of the two planets' moieties (half-orbs) — Lilly assigns orbs to planets, not to aspects. No orb table is duplicated. Each cell also carries the whole-sign aspect (sign-to-sign, degree-independent) and whether it is applying or separating. The grid is symmetric: swapping A and B transposes it and preserves every aspect and orb.

The orbs, stated plainly

The moieties used are Lilly's primary set (Sun 15°, Moon 12°, Jupiter/Saturn 9°, Mars/Mercury/Venus 7° — halved and summed for the allowance). This is generous by modern lights: contemporary synastry typically uses tighter aspect-based orbs (≈8° conjunction, 6° square/trine, 4° sextile). Both readings are honest; the choice is stated rather than hidden, and the whole-sign column is orb-free. Natal angles carry no moiety, so the default grid is the seven planets.

The classical core vs the modern grid

Only one synastry rule is genuinely classical and citable: Ptolemy, Tetrabiblos IV.5 — "Marriages… are lasting when in both the genitures the luminaries happen to be in harmonious aspect, that is, in trine or in sextile with one another, and particularly when this comes about by exchange." So the luminary interaspects (each chart's Sun/Moon to the other's) are surfaced as the cited kernel, highlighted in the grid. The full planet-by-planet matrix is a modern convention; it is provided but labelled modern. Dorotheus, Carmen Bk II treats marriage significations. The historical bridge between the classical luminary rule and the modern grid is C. G. Jung's 1952 marriage experiment (CW 8 §§872–915), which tallied cross-aspects between spouses — and found no statistical significance. Our Jung wing re-runs that experiment honestly and shows the null result: finding a striking cross-aspect here is what chance looks like, not a verdict on any relationship.

House overlays

Each person's seven planets are dropped into the other's Regiomontanus houses (the house a longitude falls in, by the same houseOf the natal tools use) — traditionally, the areas of the host's life the guest's planet animates. Each set of seven sums to seven placements, both directions.

What is honestly out of scope

The grid does not compute the composite or Davison midpoint charts, weight aspects by reception or dignity, or read the 7th-house and its lord across the two figures — the layers a traditional marriage judgement (Dorotheus II; Lilly Bk III) would demand. And no cross-aspect count means anything: it is a symbolic comparison of no demonstrated validity.

A strong, honest caveat None of this measures compatibility or predicts a relationship. The grid is exact geometry inside a symbolic convention that has been tested and found null — Jung's own marriage experiment, re-run in our Jung wing, shows it. It is offered as history and structure, cited to Ptolemy for its one classical rule and labelled modern for the rest. Interesting, not guidance. See Sources & Science.
