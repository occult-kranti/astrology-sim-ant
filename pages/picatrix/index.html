Picatrix — Astrological Magic

The Picatrix (Arabic Ghāyat al-Ḥakīm, "The Goal of the Wise", c. 11th century; Latin from 1256) is the great medieval handbook of astrological talismanic magic. It shares Lilly's machinery — signs, planets, dignities, hours — and adds four reference systems for choosing when and how to act: the mansions of the Moon, the decan faces, the Behenian fixed stars and the per-planet correspondences. Each is computed here on the same verified astronomical engine (≈1 arc-minute).

Honest note This material is presented strictly as a historical and cultural artefact — the documented practice of medieval and Renaissance magicians. It is described, not prescribed. Astrology and talismanic magic have no demonstrated efficacy; some historical recipes name substances that are toxic or illegal and are recorded for study only, never as instruction. See Sources & Science.

The 28 Mansions of the Moon

The Moon's tropical lunar zodiac — twenty-eight stations of 12°51′ each from 0° Aries — with the traditional magical use of each, after Agrippa (II.33) and Picatrix (I.4). Shows the Moon's mansion right now.

The 36 Decan Faces

Each sign's three 10° faces, their Chaldean planetary rulers (reused from Lilly's dignity table) and the talismanic images of Agrippa (II.37), with the Picatrix (II.11) variants flagged where they diverge.

Computes: the face & image for any degree · the Sun's face today.

 ✦Picatrix

The 15 Behenian Fixed Stars

The medieval set of fifteen potent stars (Hermes/Agrippa) — nature, stone, plant and use — with longitudes precessed to the current year. Alkaid is the canonical fifteenth; Fomalhaut is shown apart as a modern addition. Lists any star conjunct a planet right now.

Computes: live star longitudes (precession) · current planet–star contacts.

 ♀Picatrix

Planetary Correspondences

For each of the seven planets: the petitions it governs and its suffumigation, colour, metal, stone and named spirits — the raw material of talismanic election. The three historical spirit-name systems are kept distinct.

Computes: (reference) the correspondence set for any planet.

How Picatrix joins Lilly's astrology

Picatrix presupposes the same chart Lilly casts. The plan of this edition is to bridge the two with an Election Engine (in progress): given an aim, it judges the moment by Lilly's own rules of dignity, the planetary hour and the condition of the Moon, then reports the relevant mansion, face and star contacts above.
