Picatrix — Astrological Magic

The Picatrix (Arabic Ghāyat al-Ḥakīm, "The Goal of the Wise", c. 11th century; Latin from 1256) is the great medieval handbook of astrological talismanic magic. It shares Lilly's machinery — signs, planets, dignities, hours — and adds four reference systems for choosing when and how to act: the mansions of the Moon, the decan faces, the Behenian fixed stars and the per-planet correspondences. Each is computed here on the same verified astronomical engine (≈1 arc-minute).

Astronomy — verified Doctrine — historical Predictive validity — none demonstrated
Honest note This material is presented strictly as a historical and cultural artefact — the documented practice of medieval and Renaissance magicians. It is described, not prescribed. Astrology and talismanic magic have no demonstrated efficacy; some historical recipes name substances that are toxic or illegal and are recorded for study only, never as instruction.
