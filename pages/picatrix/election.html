Election — choosing the moment

Pick an aim, a moment and a place, and this tool auto-judges the moment for that work by William Lilly's essential and accidental dignities, the planetary hour and day, the Moon's condition — her phase, speed, mansion, via combusta and dispositor — the decan faces of the luminaries and the Behenian fixed stars in contact, and then scans the next three days to find the best upcoming windows. Every judgement is cited to its source.

Honest note The astronomy is real and reproducible — positions come from a verified engine — but the electional rules are the historical practice of Lilly, the Picatrix and Agrippa, presented strictly for historical study. Astrology has no demonstrated predictive validity. See Sources & Science.

How it works: see the step-by-step explanation of election scoring and the Moon's condition.

How to read the verdict This tool ranks a moment by weighted testimony — it is not a hard-constraint elector. A green verdict means a high score, not a guarantee; the testimonies trade off, so a strong score can still sit over a real impediment (a combust or retrograde ruler). Read the cited breakdown below, and treat the green/amber/red badge as a gravity scale, not the planetary/talisman colours.
