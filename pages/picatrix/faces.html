The 36 Decan Faces

Each zodiac sign divides into three 10° faces (decans). Every face has a ruling planet in the Chaldean order and a traditional magical image drawn from Agrippa's Three Books of Occult Philosophy II.37 — the Picatrix II.11 carries a parallel image-set that varies on a few faces (flagged in-line below). These images were used in talismanic magic.

Honest note The face boundaries and rulers are exact and reproducible; the images are historical magical iconography, described here for study, not prescribed — astrology and talismanic magic have no demonstrated validity. See Sources & Science.

Agrippa (Three Books II.37) and the Picatrix (II.11) descend from different recensions of the Greek/Arabic decan lore and diverge on several faces. Where they differ, the Agrippa image is shown as the primary entry and the Picatrix variant is given beneath it; we do not silently resolve the divergence.
