Planetary Magic — Correspondences

In the Picatrix and Cornelius Agrippa, each of the seven planets governs particular petitions and is approached with its matching suffumigations, colours, metals, stones and named spirits. This is the raw material of talismanic election — the table from which a medieval or Renaissance practitioner would have chosen what to burn, wear and inscribe under a given planet.

Honest note This page records historical magical practice for study — it is described, never prescribed. The correspondences are reproduced as the sources give them; astrology and talismanic magic have no demonstrated validity. Some historical recipes name substances that are toxic or illegal (for example opium, or "blood" in martial recipes); these are listed because the texts list them, and are in no way recommended. The seven planets

Listed in Chaldean order (slowest to fastest). Each card gives the planet's petitions and its talismanic correspondences, with the spirit-name systems kept separate (see the note below).

Why the spirit names are not merged

The historical material carries three distinct spirit-name systems, and the sources keep them apart — so this reference does too, and does not collapse them into a single "spirit of the planet":

  • Picatrix prayer-angel — the angel addressed in the planetary prayer of Picatrix Book III.7 (the Sun has none; it is approached directly).
  • Agrippa's Angel — the planetary Angel of Agrippa's Three Books of Occult Philosophy, Book II.
  • Agrippa's Intelligence — the planetary Intelligence (the ordering, benevolent power), distinct from both the Angel and the Spirit.
  • Agrippa's Spirit — the planetary Spirit (the "demon" name) of the same triad.

Conflating these would misrepresent the texts: a Picatrix prayer-angel is not Agrippa's Angel, and within Agrippa the Angel, Intelligence and Spirit are three separate names with separate roles.
