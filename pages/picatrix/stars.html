The 15 Behenian Fixed Stars

The medieval set of 15 powerful fixed stars (attributed to Hermes and transmitted by Cornelius Agrippa in Three Books of Occult Philosophy II.47) used in talismanic magic — each star carries a planetary nature and a matched stone, plant and traditional use. Because fixed-star positions precess (≈50.3″ per year, roughly 1° every 72 years), this page does not use a frozen catalog: each star's ecliptic longitude is precessed live to the current year.

Honest note The mathematics is real and reproducible — each longitude is precessed from a catalog epoch by a plain, fixed rate, and the live conjunction check uses a verified astronomical engine (≈1 arc-minute). The magical natures and uses are the historical interpretations of Hermes and Agrippa, presented strictly for historical study — astrology has no demonstrated predictive validity. See Sources & Science.
Accuracy The canonical 15th Behenian star is Alkaid (Hermes / Agrippa). Fomalhaut is one of the four Royal stars and is not one of the historical 15 — it entered the list only as a later substitute, so it is shown separately below as a modern addition, never counted among the canonical fifteen.
