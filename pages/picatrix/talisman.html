Choose an aim and a moment; this guided flow elects the time, gathers the historical correspondences, sets the Moon's mansion and the decan image, and assembles the numbered ritual steps into an end-result recipe card — every line cited, all computed on the verified engine.

Honest note This is presented strictly as a historical and cultural artefact — the documented practice of medieval and Renaissance magicians — described, not prescribed. Talismanic magic has no demonstrated efficacy; some historical recipes name materials that are toxic or illegal and are recorded for study only, never as instruction. See Sources & Science.

A worked example — a Venus talisman for concord

The classic illustration: a talisman of Venus for love & concord (Picatrix III, Venus).

  1. Aim & planet: concord → Venus.
  2. Elect the time: the day and hour of Venus, the Moon waxing and swift, not void-of-course and not in the via combusta (Spica exempt), Venus dignified and free of Mars/Saturn, a benefic on an angle. The engine scans the next 72 hours and returns the best window.
  3. Set the Moon: prefer a mansion of love/concord (e.g. Mansion 13, Al Awwa); a decan face of Venus; Spica or Sirius conjunct Venus or the Moon if available.
  4. Materials (historical): suffumigation of aloes, mastic & roses; colour white; metal copper; stone emerald.
  5. Powers (kept distinct): Picatrix prayer-angel Beyteyl; Agrippa Angel Haniel, Intelligence Hagiel, Spirit Kedemel.
  6. Design: engrave the image of Venus in copper at the elected hour.
