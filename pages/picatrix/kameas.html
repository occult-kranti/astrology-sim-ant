The planetary kameas — seven magic squares & their sigils

Agrippa's "tables of the planets" (Three Books of Occult Philosophy II.22): seven magic squares, from Saturn's 3×3 to the Moon's 9×9, each cell-pattern summing alike in every row, column and diagonal — real, checkable mathematics that the tradition read as the planets' "divine numbers." From these squares the magicians derived the planetary seals and traced the sigils of spirit-names — a line drawn cell to cell through a name's letter-values, begun with a circle and ended with a bar. How the tradition did it

Agrippa prints the finished seals and characters beside each square but withholds the method — "the wise searcher … shall easily find out." The key he leaves is Book III ch. 30: the Hebrew alphabet laid on the nine chambers of aiq bekar (units 1–9, tens 10–90, hundreds 100–900), so any name becomes a run of numbers; values beyond the square's range collapse to their chamber (300 → 3), and the line passes cell to cell — a circle where it begins, a bar where it ends, a small wave where a cell repeats. The intelligence of each planet (its "good" mover) and its spirit (the raw daemon) bear gematria values keyed to the square itself — Nachiel 111 is the Sun-square's line-sum, Sorath 666 its total. The Latin letter-values offered above are a modern convenience for study; the historical practice worked on the Hebrew spellings.
