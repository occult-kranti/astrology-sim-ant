Verified & reviewed

The library

Books, journal articles, and reputable web resources on Hermeticism, magic, alchemy, and the occult — each tagged by source type so you always know how solid the ground is beneath a claim.

The reliability tiers Scholarly an academic press or peer-reviewed work — the most reliable tier · Journal a peer-reviewed article or academic journal · Web a reputable but non-peer-reviewed site — useful for primary texts and orientation, best cross-checked against the scholarly sources. Everything listed is chosen for reliability; "bloodline / NWO" content is deliberately excluded except as the object of study in the timeline.

Ancient roots — Mesopotamia, Egypt & the Greco-Roman papyri

  • ScholarlyFrancesca Rochberg, In the Path of the Moon: Babylonian Celestial Divination and Its Legacy. Brill, 2010.
  • ScholarlyHans Dieter Betz (ed.), The Greek Magical Papyri in Translation, Including the Demotic Spells. University of Chicago Press, 1986 (2nd ed. 1992).
  • ScholarlyChristopher A. Faraone & Sofía Torallas Tovar (eds.), Greek and Egyptian Magical Formularies, vol. I. California Classical Studies, 2022.
  • ScholarlyGeraldine Pinch, Magic in Ancient Egypt. British Museum Press / University of Texas Press, 1994.

The Hermetica & the field

  • ScholarlyBrian P. Copenhaver, Hermetica: The Greek Corpus Hermeticum and the Latin Asclepius. Cambridge University Press, 1992.
  • ScholarlyFlorian Ebeling, The Secret History of Hermes Trismegistus. Cornell University Press, 2007.
  • ScholarlyWouter J. Hanegraaff, Esotericism and the Academy: Rejected Knowledge in Western Culture. Cambridge University Press, 2012.
  • ScholarlyAntoine Faivre, Access to Western Esotericism. SUNY Press, 1994.
  • ScholarlyFrances A. Yates, Giordano Bruno and the Hermetic Tradition. University of Chicago Press, 1964 — foundational but substantially revised since.

Alchemy

  • ScholarlyLawrence M. Principe, The Secrets of Alchemy. University of Chicago Press, 2013 — the leading modern survey.
  • ScholarlyWilliam R. Newman, Newton the Alchemist. Princeton University Press, 2019.
  • JournalLawrence M. Principe & William R. Newman, “Some Problems with the Historiography of Alchemy.” In Secrets of Nature, MIT Press, 2001.

Arabic transmission, the Sabians & the Picatrix

  • ScholarlyKevin van Bladel, The Arabic Hermes: From Pagan Sage to Prophet of Science. Oxford University Press, 2009.
  • JournalDavid Pingree, “The Ṣābians of Ḥarrān and the Classical Tradition.” International Journal of the Classical Tradition 9.1, 2002.
  • JournalDavid Pingree, “Between the Ghāya and Picatrix I: The Spanish Version.” Journal of the Warburg and Courtauld Institutes 44, 1981 — the scholarly spine of this site's Picatrix wing.

Grimoires & John Dee

  • ScholarlyOwen Davies, Grimoires: A History of Magic Books. Oxford University Press, 2009.
  • ScholarlyDeborah E. Harkness, John Dee's Conversations with Angels: Cabala, Alchemy, and the End of Nature. Cambridge University Press, 1999.
  • ScholarlyNicholas H. Clulee, John Dee's Natural Philosophy: Between Science and Religion. Routledge, 1988.
  • ScholarlyGyörgy E. Szőnyi, John Dee's Occultism: Magical Exaltation through Powerful Signs. SUNY Press, 2004.
  • ScholarlyDonald C. Laycock, The Complete Enochian Dictionary. Weiser, rev. 1994 — sceptical linguistic analysis of "Enochian."

Peer-reviewed journals & articles

  • JournalAries: Journal for the Study of Western Esotericism. Brill — the principal peer-reviewed journal of the field.
  • JournalCorrespondences: Journal for the Study of Esotericism. Open-access, peer-reviewed.
  • JournalWouter J. Hanegraaff, “Beyond the Yates Paradigm.” Aries 1.1, 2001 — the standard critique of the Yates thesis.
  • JournalTeresa Burns, on the John Dee "007" legend. Journal of the Western Mystery Tradition, 2010 — debunks the spy-signature myth.

Reputable web resources & primary-text archives

  • WebTwilit Grotto: Esoteric Archives (J. H. Peterson) — meticulously edited Dee diaries, Enochian material, and grimoires.
  • WebThe Hermetic Library — large, long-running archive of Hermetic, Thelemic, and ceremonial-magic texts.
  • WebSacred-Texts: Esoteric & Occult — G. R. S. Mead's Hermetica and other public-domain editions.
  • WebESSWE — the European Society for the Study of Western Esotericism; conferences and resources.
  • WebInternet Archive — scanned early editions of Agrippa, the Rosicrucian manifestos, Lévi, and more.
Method note The reliability tag on each entry is the point: scholarly and peer-reviewed work carries the most weight, web archives are valuable for primary texts but should be cross-checked, and conspiracy material is studied in the timeline rather than cited as fact.
